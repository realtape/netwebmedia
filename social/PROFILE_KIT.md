## SECTION 2 — Visual Template Set

### Designer notes before you start

All templates share these constraints:
- Background is always `#010F3B` (navy) unless a specific template says otherwise
- Orange `#FF671F` is reserved for accents, CTAs, highlights, and data callouts — never use it as a full background
- White `#FFFFFF` is the primary text color on dark backgrounds
- Never use gradients from the brand token set as a full background field — use them only for headline text treatment or small accent elements
- Minimum body text size: 24px at 1080px wide (scales proportionally)
- All templates must include the brand locker (see Section 5) in the bottom-right corner

---

### 2.1 Stat Card

**Formats:** 1080x1080 (IG square, FB), 1600x900 (X, used as an attached image)

**Purpose:** Anchor a single data claim. Forces a stop-scroll on the feed.

**Layout — 1080x1080**

The canvas is divided into three horizontal bands:

**Band 1 (top, ~15% of height):** Brand locker only. Left-aligned NWM wordmark in white. Right-aligned platform handle in muted text (`#C8D4E6`, Inter 400, 18px). No other elements here.

**Band 2 (center, ~60% of height):** The stat lives here. Full vertical centering within the band.
- The number/stat itself: Poppins 700, minimum 96px, color `#FF671F` (orange). If the stat is a percentage (e.g., "73%"), the percentage sign is the same weight but 60% of the number's size and sits at top-right of the number, superscript style.
- One-line label below the number: Inter 500, 32px, `#FFFFFF`. No more than 8 words. If it won't fit in 8 words, the brief is wrong — shorten it.
- Source line (optional): Inter 400, 18px, `#C8D4E6`. "Source: [Name], [Year]." Right-aligned under the label.

**Band 3 (bottom, ~25% of height):** The payoff line and CTA.
- Payoff: Inter 500, 24px, `#C8D4E6`. One sentence connecting the stat to the prospect's pain. Example: "If you're not in the answer, you don't exist to that buyer."
- CTA pill: Orange `#FF671F` background, white text, Inter 600, 20px, pill-shaped (border-radius 9999px). Text: "Free Audit — netwebmedia.com". Right-aligned. Minimum pill width: 320px. Minimum height: 56px. Left padding 28px, right padding 28px.

**Layout — 1600x900 (X format)**

Same three-band logic but the bands are left-aligned content blocks with the right 30% of canvas used as a bleed zone:
- Right bleed: solid `#FF671F` vertical stripe, 12px wide, full height. This is the only decorative element on the 1600x900 format. It signals the brand immediately at small thumbnail size in X feeds.
- All text content is left-aligned within the left 70% of the canvas.
- Stat number: Poppins 700, 120px minimum.
- The brand locker anchors bottom-left on this format (not bottom-right), to avoid collision with the bleed stripe.

**Color use rule:** The orange is used ONCE on the stat card — on the number. The CTA pill is also orange. There is no third orange element. If a third orange element seems necessary, you are overdesigning.

### 2.2 Carousel Slide — Instagram (1080x1350)

**Purpose:** Teach something in 8 steps. The carousel is the deepest content format on Instagram. It earns saves, not just likes.

**Overall narrative arc (8 slides):**

| Slide | Type | Purpose |
|-------|------|---------|
| 1 | Cover | Hook + promise. Viewer commits to swiping. |
| 2 | Problem statement | Validate the pain. One sentence, large. |
| 3 | Why it happens | Root cause. Short. Positions us as technical. |
| 4–7 | Solution steps (4 slides) | One actionable point per slide. Numbered. |
| 8 | CTA | Single ask. Reinforce handle + URL. |

**Slide 1 (Cover) layout:**
- Background: `#010F3B`
- Top third: hook text in Poppins 700, 52–64px, white. Maximum 2 lines. Line 1 is the provocative statement. Line 2 (if needed) is the specific outcome.
- Middle: thin 2px horizontal rule in `#FF671F`, full width with 64px margin on each side.
- Bottom third: "Swipe to see all [X]" in Inter 400, 22px, `#C8D4E6`, left-aligned. Below it: brand locker.
- Top-right corner: slide number indicator — not on slide 1. First visible on slide 2.

**Slides 2–7 (Content) layout:**
- Background: alternates. Odd slides: `#010F3B`. Even slides: `#011550` (slightly lighter navy — visible but subtle). This helps the viewer feel progression without a jarring visual break.
- Slide number: top-right, Inter 600, 20px, `#FF671F`. Format: "02 / 08"
- Content area starts at y=160px from top.
- Step number (slides 4–7 only): Poppins 700, 80px, `#FF671F`, ~15% opacity, positioned as a background watermark behind the text, top-left of content area. This adds visual interest without competing with the readable text.
- Headline: Poppins 600, 42–48px, `#FFFFFF`. Max 2 lines.
- Body copy: Inter 400, 26px, `#C8D4E6`. Max 3 lines, ~60 characters per line. If copy exceeds this, it needs editing — not a smaller font.
- Divider between headline and body: 1px line, `#FF671F`, 120px wide, left-aligned.
- No images, no icons, no decorative elements beyond the step watermark. Text carries the design.

**Slide 8 (CTA) layout:**
- Background: `#FF671F` (the only slide with an orange background in the entire kit)
- This inversion signals the end and the ask. It must feel different.
- Headline: Poppins 700, 52px, `#010F3B` (navy text on orange — reversed). "Book Your Free AI Audit."
- Sub: Inter 400, 26px, `#010F3B`. "We'll show you exactly where you're invisible — and how to fix it."
- URL: Inter 700, 28px, `#010F3B`, underlined visually. "netwebmedia.com"
- Handle: "@netwebmedia" in Inter 500, 22px, `#010F3B`
- Brand locker: navy version (see Section 5 for inverted locker spec).

**Transition logic:**
Swipe-to-swipe continuity is maintained by: consistent headline position (same y-coordinate on every content slide), consistent type scale, and the slide counter in the same corner position every slide. The viewer's eye trains on the pattern by slide 3 and reads faster. Do not break the grid between slides.

**Text density rule:** If you cannot read a slide comfortably at arm's length from your phone screen, the slide has too much text. Cut. Every word that survives the cut has to earn its place.

### 2.4 Reel / TikTok Vertical Thumbnail (9:16 — 1080x1920)

**Purpose:** The thumbnail/cover frame is the poster for the video. It determines whether someone taps. It is not a design opportunity — it is a conversion problem.

**Layout:**

- Background: Carlos's face, center-frame, cropped from mid-chest up. He fills 60–70% of the frame height. Expression is direct, not performative. Eyes are at approximately y=35% from the top of the frame. This is the visual anchor.
- Color treatment: a subtle `#010F3B` navy overlay at 40–50% opacity over the full frame, except a vertical strip from Carlos's face that gets 0% overlay. This makes his face pop while unifying the frame's color temperature with brand.
- Hook text: Poppins 700, 52–60px, `#FFFFFF`, placed in the upper 25% of the frame. Maximum 7 words. No filler words ("this," "these," "you need to know"). Example: "ChatGPT Doesn't Know You Exist." The text sits on a semi-transparent navy bar (`#010F3B` at 70% opacity) to ensure legibility over any background.
- Bottom brand locker: positioned at y=88% from top, spanning the full width with 40px left/right padding. Left: "@netwebmedia" in white, Inter 600, 24px. Right: "netwebmedia.com" in orange, Inter 600, 24px.
- No CTA button on the thumbnail. The hook text does the job.

**What never appears on a Reel/TikTok thumbnail:**
- Text that requires more than 2 seconds to read
- The NWM logo large — the brand locker at the bottom is sufficient
- Stock imagery or AI-generated backgrounds
- Multiple competing color calls (one accent, one)

**Pattern A — Face-focused (for opinion, commentary, personal audit videos)**

Where Carlos's figure overlaps the orange half, his silhouette should be clean — shoot on a dark or neutral background for easy post-production cutout.

Text sits in the orange right half:

- Below main line: a thin 3px white rule, 160px wide.

Brand locker: bottom-left corner of the frame, on Carlos's side, in white (since the background there is dark/Carlos's jacket).

**Pattern B — Data-focused (for breakdown, audit, explainer videos)**

Full navy background `#010F3B`. No face.

- Large number or icon in `#FF671F`, centered, Poppins 700, 140px+.
- Label below number: Inter 600, 36px, `#FFFFFF`.
- Below label: the specific hook, 2 lines max, Poppins 600, 48px, `#FFFFFF`.
- Bottom-right: brand locker.

**CTR principles applied:**
- Thumbnails are read at approximately 168x94px in most feed contexts. Every element must be identifiable at that size. Test by shrinking your design to 168px wide before export.
- Orange occupies a defined region, not a random accent — the eye goes there first. Headline sits in orange area (Pattern A) or orange is the headline (Pattern B).
- Carlos's face in Pattern A should show a clear, readable expression — not neutral, not exaggerated. A slight forward lean reads as authority at small sizes.

## SECTION 4 — Posting Format Rules

### 4.1 When to use the orange CTA button

Use the orange pill/button (`#FF671F` background, white text, border-radius 9999px) when:
- The post has a direct response goal: book a call, download something, visit a URL.
- The post is a stat card, carousel CTA slide, or OG card.
- The action is the last thing in the visual hierarchy.

Rules for the orange button:
- One per asset. Never two orange CTAs competing.
- Text is action-first: "Book Free Audit," "See Pricing," "Read the Breakdown." Not "Click Here" or "Learn More."
- Minimum size: 48px height, 20px horizontal padding on each side of the text. The button must be legible and tappable.

### 4.2 When to use the navy pill

Use the navy pill (`#010F3B` background, white text, `#FF671F` border, 2px) when:
- The post is educational or informational — not asking for a direct action.
- You want to label a post type: "AI SEARCH," "CASE STUDY," "TOOL BREAKDOWN." These are category tags, not CTAs.
- The orange button already appears on the same asset and you need a secondary label.

### 4.3 When to use headshot vs logo mark vs pure text

**Headshot (Carlos):**
- Quote cards — always
- Reel / TikTok thumbnails — always
- YouTube Pattern A thumbnails — always
- Any post where the content is a personal opinion, observation, or direct address

**Logo mark / Wordmark:**
- Stat cards
- Carousel covers
- OG cards
- YouTube Pattern B thumbnails
- Any post where the content is a brand claim, service description, or aggregate data

**Pure text:**
- X posts that are thread starters — no visual needed if the hook is strong enough
- Short-form TikTok/Reel overlays where the face is the visual and text is the caption layer only
- Brand voice moments where imagery would dilute the message

### 4.4 What NEVER to post

The following are hard prohibitions. They apply to Carlos's account and any account posting as or for NetWebMedia.

**No stock photography.**
Stock images read as generic. The brand is built on specificity and founder-led authenticity. A stock photo of "a businessman at a laptop" is the visual equivalent of "we help businesses grow." It signals exactly nothing about who we are.

**No lorem ipsum.**
If a template goes to a designer or to Canva with placeholder text still visible, it does not get published. Placeholder text means the design has not been completed. Publication means the design is done.

**No generic AI-generated "robot" imagery.**
A neon robot head, a glowing circuit board, or a CGI humanoid holding a briefcase are not "AI marketing." They are the laziest visual shorthand in the category. Every competitor has used them. They do not differentiate — they blend into noise. We write and design about AI. We do not decorate with clichés of AI.

**No emojis in graphic text.**
Emojis in captions are a platform-native choice Carlos may make. Emojis in designed graphics are off-brand. They conflict with the premium, precise visual language.

**No white backgrounds.**
All designed assets use navy as the base. A white-background post in the grid reads as a mistake, not a style choice. If content requires a light background for legibility (e.g., a screen recording), frame it inside a navy card with generous padding.

**No unattributed statistics.**
If a stat appears on a card, there is a source line. Even if it is a NetWebMedia internal figure ("Based on 40 SMB audits, Q1 2026"), it is attributed. Unattributed stats look fabricated.

## SECTION 5 — Brand Locker

### Definition

The brand locker is a compact, consistent lockup that appears in every single designed asset. It is non-negotiable — like a watermark, but designed.

### Anatomy

The locker is a horizontal lockup, left-to-right:

1. NWM wordmark (the "NWM" letterform only, no full URL): white, Poppins 700, 18px
2. 1px vertical divider: `#FF671F`, 16px tall, vertically centered, 8px margin on each side
3. Handle: "@netwebmedia", Inter 400, 16px, `#C8D4E6`

Total approximate width: 200–220px. Total height: 24px.

This is the standard (light) locker — white text on dark backgrounds.

### Inverted locker

Used only on orange-background assets (Carousel Slide 8):
- NWM wordmark: `#010F3B` (navy), Poppins 700, 18px
- Divider: `#010F3B` at 40% opacity
- Handle: `#010F3B`, Inter 400, 16px

### Placement rules

| Format | Locker position |
|--------|----------------|
| 1080x1080 | Bottom-right corner, 32px from right edge, 32px from bottom |
| 1080x1350 | Bottom-right corner, 32px from right edge, 40px from bottom |
| 1920x1080 (Reel cover) | Bottom of frame, full-width strip, left side = handle, right side = URL (this replaces the compact locker with an expanded footer treatment — see Section 2.4) |
| 1280x720 (YT thumb) | Bottom-left corner, 24px from left, 24px from bottom |
| 1600x900 (X) | Bottom-left corner, 24px from left, 24px from bottom |
| 1200x630 (OG card) | Bottom-left of text column, 24px from left, 24px from bottom |
| 1500x500 (X banner) | Integrated into layout, not a corner element — see Section 3.2 |

### What the brand locker is NOT

- It is not a large NWM logo dominating the frame
- It is not a white box or badge behind it — it sits directly on the background
- It is not optional on any designed deliverable
- It is not replaced by the URL alone

### Scaling rule

The locker is designed at the sizes above for 1080px-wide canvases. For larger canvases (1280px, 1600px, 2560px), scale proportionally: locker height stays at 24px for anything under 1500px wide. For 1600px+ wide canvases, scale up to 28–32px locker height. Never scale beyond 32px — it stops reading as a locker and starts reading as a logo.
