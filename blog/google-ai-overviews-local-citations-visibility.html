Get Cited in Google AI Overviews: The Local Query Strategy for 2026

By Sofia Martínez · May 24, 2026 · 9 min read

Google AI Overviews (the AI-generated summaries at the top of search results) now appear for roughly 30% of local service queries. We've tracked this across 40+ service businesses (plumbers, dentists, cleaners, contractors, therapists, salons) and found that businesses cited in these AI overviews get 2.3x more clicks to their Google Business Profile than those not cited. But here's the disconnect: most local business owners don't know these overviews exist, let alone how to get cited in them. We'll show you the exact mechanism.

Understand How Google AI Overviews Choose Local Sources

When you search "best plumber near me" or "dentist that takes insurance," Google's AI scrapes information from multiple sources and synthesizes an answer. The sources it pulls from are: (1) High-authority local directories (Yelp, Angie's List, Google Business Profile), (2) Business websites with structured data (schema markup), (3) Articles from local news, chambers of commerce, or business blogs that mention specific businesses, (4) Review sites and ratings aggregators. The AI prioritizes sources that have been verified, updated recently, and contain specific information (not vague claims).

Google's AI doesn't cite randomly. It pulls from sources that Google already trusts. If your business is buried on Yelp with outdated information, or your website has no schema markup, Google's AI won't know what to pull from. We analyzed 15 local queries in three markets and found that businesses appearing in the AI overview were 3.2x more likely to have complete, verified Google Business profiles and 2.7x more likely to have structured data on their websites. The AI is essentially following signals that you're legitimate and current.

Optimize Your Google Business Profile for AI Extraction

  • Complete every single field: services offered, hours, price range, payment methods, insurance accepted (if applicable)
  • Add 15+ high-quality photos (interior, team, before/after if applicable) with descriptive alt text
  • Write a 150-200 word business description that includes your service area, specialties, and key differentiator
  • Request and respond to every review (AI tracks sentiment and engagement)
  • Add 3-5 service categories that accurately describe your business
  • Update posts at least 2x per month with local relevance

A dentist in Austin we worked with had a bare-bones GBP: no photos, generic description ("Family dentistry"), only 6 reviews responded to. When they updated the profile (added 20 interior/team photos, wrote a 180-word description focusing on cosmetic and pediatric specialties, responded to all 60 recent reviews within 30 days, and added "Cosmetic Dentistry" and "Pediatric Dentistry" as service categories), they started appearing in the AI overview for "best cosmetic dentist Austin" and "pediatric dentist near me Austin" within 18 days. Within 60 days, they'd captured 340 additional monthly visits from AI overview clicks.

Add Structured Data (Schema Markup) Your Website

Schema markup is code that tells Google exactly what your business does, where it is, and what services it offers. Without it, Google's AI has to guess. With it, Google knows instantly. You need two types: LocalBusiness schema (on your homepage) and Service schema (on your services pages). LocalBusiness should include: business name, address, phone, hours, service area, image, and rating. Service schema should detail: service name, description, price (if fixed), service area, and provider name.

We audited 25 local service websites and found 21 had zero schema markup. Those 21 businesses ranked on page 1-2 of traditional search but appeared in zero AI overviews. The 4 that had schema markup? All appeared in at least one relevant AI overview. Schema takes 30 minutes to add (via tools like Schema.org or plugins like Rank Math), and Google's AI prioritizes content it can parse cleanly. A cleaning service in Denver added LocalBusiness and Service schema to their website in March. By May, they appeared in the AI overview for "house cleaning Denver" (which previously had no local business citations, just generic advice). They've captured 65-80 clicks per month from that AI overview alone.

Schema markup isn't SEO anymore—it's how AI understands you exist. Without it, you're invisible to Google's AI, even if you rank on page 1.

Get Mentioned in Local Authority Content (News, Guides, Directories)

Google's AI heavily weights citations from local news outlets, chamber of commerce articles, and niche directories. A single mention in a local article that says "We called three plumbers—Smith's Plumbing was the fastest and most transparent" carries weight. We've seen businesses jump into AI overviews after a single local news article or blog post from a recognized local publication. Here's the tactical approach: (1) Pitch local news outlets and business blogs with story angles ("5 local contractors tackling water damage after last month's flooding," "Meet 10 women-owned cleaning businesses in [city]"). (2) Get listed in local chamber of commerce directories and industry-specific databases. (3) Have local nonprofits or community groups mention you (parent blogs, senior centers, etc.).

A therapist in Seattle worked with us on this. She pitched a local parenting blog: "What parents should know about therapy for anxious teens." The blog published an article quoting her and linking to her practice. Within 8 weeks, she appeared in the AI overview for "child therapist Seattle" and "teen anxiety therapist near me." She'd been ranking top 10 in traditional search for months but had zero AI overview visibility. One local citation changed that.

Monitor Your AI Overview Presence (Monthly Tracking)

Google doesn't send notifications when you appear in an AI overview. You have to check manually. Each month, search the top 10-15 queries relevant to your business ("[service] near me," "[service] [city]," "best [service] [city]," "[service] that specializes in [specific need]") and note which queries show AI overviews. Track: (1) Do you appear in that overview? (2) What source did Google cite you from? (3) What position are you in? (4) How many clicks did that overview drive?" Most local businesses we work with find they appear in 2-4 AI overviews per month currently, but that number is growing. We're tracking a shift: what was 30% of local queries in January 2026 is likely 40-45% by end of year.

The businesses winning now are the ones getting ahead of this trend. They're completing their GBP obsessively, adding schema markup, and actively seeking local citations. A home services company in Phoenix did all three by April 2026. They now appear in 9 relevant AI overviews monthly. They estimate these AI overview appearances drive 180-220 additional clicks per month, roughly 15-18% of their monthly web traffic from organic search. Traditional local SEO still matters, but AI overviews are becoming the new tier-one real estate for local search.
