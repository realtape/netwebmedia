The 3 lines of JSON-LD most law firms are missing.

Last week we said FAQPage schema gets cited 3.2× more than text. Several of you replied: "OK, how do I add it without rewriting the site?"

Three lines. That's the answer.

The minimum FAQPage block answer engines look for:

{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "FAQPage",
  "mainEntity": [{
    "@type": "Question",
    "name": "How long does a personal-injury case take?",
    "acceptedAnswer": {
      "@type": "Answer",
      "text": "Most personal-injury cases settle in 6–12 months..."
    }
  }]
}

That's it. Drop it in a <script type="application/ld+json"> tag at the bottom of any practice-area page. No CMS surgery. No redesign. ChatGPT and Perplexity both index it within 7–14 days.

One observation we're tracking

We audited 50 law-firm practice pages this week. 43 of 50 had zero FAQPage schema. Of the 7 that did, 6 are getting cited in ChatGPT for at least one anchor query. The single firm with FAQPage that isn't cited had answer text under 40 words — answer engines pass on shallow content.

So: schema + meaningful answers (150+ words each). Both, not either.

One action to take this week

  1. Pick your top practice-area page (the one you'd want a prospect to find).
  2. List the 5 questions prospects actually ask before signing — the ones that come up on intake calls.
  3. Write a 150-word answer to each. The exact text you'd say in plain English.
  4. Wrap them in the FAQPage JSON-LD block above — one Q&A per mainEntity entry.
  5. Add to the page. Re-crawl via Google Search Console. Done.
