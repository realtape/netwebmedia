1. Why getting named by AI matters now

When someone asks ChatGPT "which marketing agency is best for my bakery?", ChatGPT names three or four companies. Those names are the new top ranking. Getting named is called AEO (Answer Engine Optimization).

Getting named by AI is not the same as ranking on Google. AI rewards:

  • Clear question-and-answer pages that an AI can pull a clean answer from.
  • Real numbers and specifics. "Saved $47,000 in the second quarter" beats "saved a lot."
  • Recent content. AI heavily ignores content more than a year old.
  • Your own data. Studies, case results, named experts — things nobody else can copy.

2. Our three-part plan

Part A: Topic clusters

5 to 8 long "pillar" pages (each 3,000 words) with 10 to 20 shorter supporting articles built around each pillar.

Part B: Question pages

Every question your sales team hears, answered on its own URL. This is gold for AI tools.

Part C: Original data

Data and case studies AI tools cannot find anywhere else. You become the place they quote.

3. How we write each month

1

Month 1: plan it.

We research keywords. We look at what your competitors rank for. We design the topic map. We lay out a 90-day writing calendar.

2

Every week: ship new content.

2 long pillar articles and 4 supporting articles per week. AI writes the first draft. A human expert from your industry edits it. An editor polishes it.

3

Every new article links to 3 to 5 older ones.

This builds authority fast. Google and AI both reward tightly linked topic clusters.

4

Every quarter: deep site check.

Site speed. Page structure. Broken links. Search engine readability. We fix everything flagged.

5. How we measure results

Your monthly scorecard covers:

  • Website visitors — total sessions, new visitors, engaged visits (from Google Analytics).
  • Google rankings — how many of your target keywords sit in the top 10, and whether they're going up or down.
  • AI mentions — we ask ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Claude 30-plus target questions every week. We track which of your URLs get named.
  • Revenue impact — what percent of your sales-qualified leads touched an article before buying.

SEO is slow. Period.

If someone promises results in 30 days, they are lying or cheating with paid links that will get you banned by Google. Real SEO takes 6 months minimum. Plan for it.

What success looks like

  • Within 90 days: 10 to 30 new articles live. First few keywords showing up in Google.
  • Within 6 months: website traffic doubled or tripled. First AI tools naming your business.
  • Within 12 months: a steady stream of inbound leads from organic search. Direct brand searches growing.

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Publishing AI drafts without human editing. Google and AI both reward unique human insight. Skip the edit and your content blends in.
  • Chasing keyword volume, not customer questions. Volume feels exciting. A page answering one question your buyer actually asks is worth ten volume chasers.
  • Giving up at month 3. Month 3 is the dip. The compounding happens from month 4 on. Keep going.
