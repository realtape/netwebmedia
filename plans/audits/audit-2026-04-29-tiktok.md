# TikTok Presence Audit — NetWebMedia
**Date:** April 29, 2026  
**Auditor:** Content Strategist  
**Status:** NO ACCOUNT · RECOMMEND DEFER  

---

## Current State
**No @netwebmedia TikTok account exists.** Web search, direct URL probes, and social handle queries across `tiktok.com` return no results. The infrastructure to *produce* short-form video exists (`video-factory/` Remotion renderer + `hyperframes/nwm-reels/` b-roll library with 60+ clips), but distribution is not in use.

---

## Strategic Fit Assessment

### The Case FOR TikTok
- **B2B adoption is real.** Codie Sanchez (2M followers, B2B finance/entrepreneurship), Clarvos (AI marketing platform), and restaurant SaaS are all active. TikTok Search is becoming a discovery engine alongside Google and ChatGPT.
- **SMB awareness is there.** 49% YoY growth in SMB adoption on TikTok Shop; 171,000 small business merchants on the platform. Over 50% of TikTok users visited a restaurant after seeing it on-platform.
- **Demographics matured.** Users aged 25–44 (business decision-makers) now comprise 65.3% of the platform. 37% have household income >$100k. Not just Gen-Z anymore.
- **Niche flywheel.** Some of NetWebMedia's 14 verticals (beauty, fitness, restaurants, events/weddings) are *native* to TikTok. Creators and salon owners already hang there.
- **Bilingual advantage.** NetWebMedia's LATAM playbook can skip the "translate-after" trap. Spanish TikTok is massive for SMBs.
- **Production debt paid.** Video-factory + b-roll library = low marginal cost to publish once cadence is set.

### The Case AGAINST TikTok
- **Wrong primary demographic.** NetWebMedia's target SMB is the restaurant owner in Phoenix (52 years old), the dental practice manager (45), the HVAC contractor (55). These people live on Facebook, WhatsApp, and email. TikTok for B2B agency services is *aspirational* — not where the buyer is shopping.
- **Noisy, unproven ROI for B2B services.** Codie Sanchez sells *ideas* (entrepreneurship, finance mindset). NetWebMedia sells *retainers* ($249–$2,499/mo). Services contracts close on trust, case studies, and track record — not viral credibility stacking.
- **Opportunity cost is high.** Google + Meta + YouTube are still under-optimized in the paid mix (45% / 20% / 25%). Organic blog posts are the true AEO lever. LinkedIn was explicitly killed by CEO directive (`0%` in plan). TikTok would be another zero-payoff channel dividing focus from proven levers.
- **Virality risk.** TikTok algorithm rewards young, entertaining, casual tone. NetWebMedia's brand is sharp, contrarian, data-driven. Forced "relatable CEO" videos feel inauthentic. Codie Sanchez works because she *is* the product.
- **Churn in metrics.** TikTok's API is hostile to SMB SaaS attribution. No pixel like Meta. No structured conversion tracking. Hard to prove CAC or tie followers → leads → customers.

---

## Recommendation: DEFER

**Decision:** Do NOT launch TikTok presence in Q2 2026.

**Rationale (1 para):**  
TikTok is a real channel for *some* B2B — mostly founder/creator brands selling mindset, courses, or entertainment. NetWebMedia is selling fractional CMO retainers to 50-year-old restaurant and contractor owners who discover agencies via Google, referrals, and editorial authority. The platform's demographics have matured, but they skew toward younger professionals and Gen-Z buyers with high disposable income — not the core $500k–$10M revenue SMB segment. Spend the constrained CEO + content bandwidth on doubling down on blog/AEO (organic CAC = $0), paid Google/Meta (proven CAC <$1k), and YouTube pre-roll (higher-intent than TikTok for SaaS). Revisit TikTok in **Q3 2026** if: (a) blog + organic reach 50k+ monthly visitors, (b) case studies are published and performing, (c) LATAM SMB pilot shows revenue lift, (d) founder bandwidth is available. Until then, it's a distraction.

## If You Changed Your Mind: Minimum Viable Cadence

**If Carlos decides TikTok adds strategic value** (e.g., founder-brand play for personal positioning, LATAM SMB discovery acceleration, or niche industry verticals like beauty/restaurants), here's the lowest-friction path:

- **Frequency:** 1–2 posts per week (60–90 seconds each)
- **Content pillars:** 
  - Behind-the-scenes (Carlos shipping features, audit clips)
  - Contrarian takes (why X agency strategy fails)
  - Niche wins (a beauty salon went from 0 citations to 47 in 60 days)
  - Tools/templates (5-min AEO audit framework)
- **Production:** Repurpose existing blog breakdowns + video-factory renders to short clips
- **Distribution:** Auto-post via Meta Business Suite or Hootsuite; no human optimization needed
- **Success metric:** NOT follower count, NOT engagement rate. Track: inbound qualified leads with "found you on TikTok" in intake form. If <5% of leads per month, pause after 12 posts and reallocate.

---

## Alternative: Smart LATAM Expansion Instead

If CEO wants to court Gen-Z/millennial LatAm SMB owners (growing cohort who *do* live on TikTok), consider:

1. **Launch TikTok in Spanish-first mode** on a secondary handle (`@netwebmedia_es` or regional variant)
2. **Hyper-niche vertical pilot:** Start with restaurants or beauty (highest TikTok penetration in LatAm SMB)
3. **Partner with 1–2 LatAm creator agencies** (low cost, high local credibility)
4. **Set 90-day revenue target:** $2k MRR from LatAm SMB, sourced via TikTok UTM. If hit, expand to other verticals. If not, kill.

This isolates TikTok risk to a single geo/niche and clarifies the real question: *Does TikTok unlock a LatAm SMB flywheel?* (vs. the US SMB question, which is "no" for now).
