1. What NWM CMS is (and isn't)

NWM CMS is a managed, AI-assisted website builder for SMBs. Think: WordPress speed, Webflow polish, HubSpot SEO tooling — bundled into one tool.

What it does well:

  • Marketing sites (homepage, services, about, blog, landing pages)
  • Bilingual content (English + Spanish out of the box; other languages on Enterprise)
  • AI-generated drafts that respect your brand voice
  • SEO + AEO optimization baked into every page
  • Forms, memberships, gated content

What it's not:

  • An ecommerce platform (use Shopify for product catalogs)
  • A general-purpose app framework (use Next.js)
  • A social network

Open netwebmedia.com/cms-demo/ — pre-loaded with a full sample site. Click any feature, nothing persists.

2. Spin up a new site

1

Pick a template

Open /cms/templates.html. 12 industry templates available (law firm, restaurant, SaaS, agency, e-com…). Click "Use this template".

2

Tell the AI about your business

Answer 8 questions: business name, one-line pitch, target customer, main services, tone, primary color, existing site URL (if any), competitor URLs (if any).

3

Let it generate

The AI writes your homepage, services pages, about page, contact page, and 3 blog posts — in your chosen language + tone. Takes ~2 minutes.

4

Review & edit

Every section is editable inline. Click any text to rewrite. Click any image to swap. Click any section to remove or reorder.

3. Pages & templates

The pages view (/cms/pages.html) lists every page on your site. Each page has:

Slug

/services/seo — the URL path.

SEO title + meta

Auto-generated, editable, AEO-scored.

Open Graph image

Auto-rendered from title + brand colors.

Status

Draft / Published / Scheduled.

Block-based editor

Every page is built from blocks: hero, features grid, testimonials, pricing, FAQ, CTA, contact form, etc. Add, remove, reorder, or duplicate. Each block has 2–4 design variants.

4. Blog + AI content writer

Open /cms/content-writer.html. This is where ~80% of your SEO content will come from.

1

Give it a topic

"10 ways AI can cut CAC for SMBs". Or paste a competitor URL and hit "outrank this".

2

Pick the format

Listicle, how-to, comparison, case study, opinion. Each format has a proven structure the AI follows.

3

Generate & edit

Draft appears in ~40s. Comes pre-formatted with H2s, internal links, FAQ schema, and Open Graph image. Edit anything inline.

4

Publish or queue

Publish now, schedule, or queue for batch publishing (1/day, 2/week, etc.).

⚠️ Always human-edit before publishing

AI drafts are 80% there. The last 20% — voice, specificity, real examples — has to come from you. Search engines reward human experience signals, and LLMs cite pages with unique insight. Don't skip this.

5. Landing pages & A/B tests

Landing pages live at /cms/landing-pages.html. Build a one-off page for a campaign in < 10 minutes:

  1. Pick a landing-page template (lead magnet, webinar, free trial, etc.)
  2. Edit headline, sub-headline, bullets, form fields
  3. Connect the form to the CRM (auto-wired if you're using NWM CRM)
  4. Publish to any URL you want: /promo/q2-campaign

A/B testing

Open /cms/ab-tests.html. Pick a page, clone it as variant B, edit what you want to test (headline? CTA color? form length?). The CMS splits traffic 50/50 and declares a winner at statistical significance.

6. SEO + AEO planner

Two tools you'll use weekly:

SEO planner (/cms/seo.html)

Enter a seed keyword. You get a keyword cluster — 1 pillar topic + 8–15 supporting topics — mapped to search volume + difficulty + intent. Click any topic to auto-draft a blog post.

AEO planner (/cms/seo-planner.html)

AEO = Answer Engine Optimization. Enter a question your customers ask ("how much does fractional CMO cost?"). You get the question restructured for LLM citation + a page template designed to be quoted by ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude.ai, and Google AI Overviews.

7. Forms & memberships

Forms (/cms/forms.html) are drag-and-drop. Each submission:

  • Lands in your CRM as a new contact (if NWM CRM is connected)
  • Fires an email to whoever you choose
  • Optionally triggers an automation sequence
  • Exports to CSV, Google Sheets, or Zapier

Memberships (/cms/memberships.html)

Gate content behind a login. Use cases: customer portals, premium blog content, downloadable resources, course access. Supports free + paid tiers (Stripe integration).

8. Bilingual publishing

Every page has a language toggle at the top. Create in English, click "translate to Spanish", review, publish. The CMS:

  • Generates hreflang tags automatically (Google understands the mirror)
  • Serves /es/page for Spanish visitors, /page for English
  • Preserves slugs where you want (/services + /es/servicios)
  • Let the AI translate, or paste your own
💡 Latino markets

If you serve US Hispanic or LATAM markets, bilingual from day one ~1.8× your addressable audience. Even if your sales team is English-only, having a Spanish marketing site compounds organic reach.

9. Launch checklist

Before you go live, the CMS auto-runs this checklist:

  • ✅ All pages have meta title + description
  • ✅ All images have alt text
  • ✅ All forms submit successfully
  • ✅ Mobile layout passes at 375px, 768px, 1440px
  • ✅ Lighthouse score > 90 on home + top 3 pages
  • ✅ GA4 + Meta Pixel + LinkedIn Pixel wired
  • ✅ Favicon + apple-touch-icon present
  • ✅ Sitemap.xml generated + submitted to Google Search Console

Fix each flag, then hit "Launch". DNS cutover typically happens within 15 minutes.

Common questions

Can I export my site to self-host?

Yes — Settings → Export. You get a static HTML bundle + CSS + JS. Host it anywhere (Netlify, Vercel, your own server). Dynamic features (forms, memberships) require staying on NWM CMS.

Does it work with my existing domain?

Yes. Point an A record at our servers (we give you the IP), add the TXT record we show you, and we issue an SSL cert automatically.

Can I use my own brand fonts?

Yes. Settings → Design → upload .woff2 files. The CMS auto-subsets them for performance.

Is my data exportable at any time?

Yes — always. Pages export as Markdown or HTML, media as a zip, contacts as CSV. No lock-in.
