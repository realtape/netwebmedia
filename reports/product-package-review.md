## 1. Current Package Assessment

### What exists today (post 2026-04-21 repricing)

| Tier | Price | Setup | What's promised | LTV / LTV:CAC | Tenure |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| CMO Lite | $249/mo | $0 | AEO + SEO strategy, monthly content calendar, SEO audit, NWM CRM, monthly async strategy note | $1,862 / 3.1x | 11 mo |
| CMO Growth | $999/mo + 12% ad mgmt (min $300) | $499 | Lite + paid ads, social posting, email automation, AI SDR, monthly async report | $9,790 / 6.5x | 14 mo |
| CMO Scale | $2,499/mo + 12% ad mgmt (min $300) | $999 | Growth + Video Factory (16 Reels), custom AI agents + voice AI, white-glove onboarding, dedicated strategist, daily Slack | $32,387 / 10.1x | 18 mo |

Annual pre-pay: 15% discount. 14-day trial. 90-day minimum. NWM CRM bundled in all tiers.

### What's working

1. **Ladder economics are clean.** A Scale client is worth ~17x a Lite client. Cohort retention on Scale (44% at 24 mo) is enterprise-SaaS-grade. The math says "sell Scale."
2. **Lite as a wedge is the right call.** $249 is now below HubSpot Starter — that's a positioning win, not a parity play. The 90-day upgrade credit ($249 toward Growth/Scale) is a real upgrade lever.
3. **Ad management fee fixed a margin leak.** 12% + $300 minimum on Growth/Scale is industry-standard and was previously missing.
4. **Bundling CRM into every tier removes a buying objection.** No "but do I also need HubSpot?" conversation.

### What's broken or weak

1. **The middle tier is doing too much.** Growth ($999) bundles paid ads, social, email automation, *and* AI SDR. That's three distinct problems collapsed into one SKU. A founder who needs ads but not SDR gets priced into a feature they won't use; a founder who needs SDR but isn't running ads has nowhere to go.
2. **"Strategy note" is the wrong delivery atom for Lite.** A monthly async note is too low-touch for someone paying $249 to feel like they bought a CMO. Retention curve confirms it — 48% by month 12 vs Growth's 62%. Lite churns because it doesn't feel like anything is happening.
3. **No clear upgrade trigger.** The $249 credit is a promo, not a product mechanic. There is no in-product moment that says "you've outgrown Lite — upgrade."
4. **Scale features are vague.** "Custom AI agents + voice AI" + "dedicated strategist" reads like agency-speak. What does the buyer actually get on day 30, 60, 90?
5. **Async-only delivery (WhatsApp + email) is not communicated as a feature.** Right now it reads like a missing feature ("no Zoom?") instead of the deliberate product choice it is. The Scale tier even contradicts this with "weekly strategy call" in the business plan.
6. **The model needs Lever A volume (~100 retainers by M12) to hit $85k MRR.** The current Lite SKU is not productized enough for self-serve. There's no checkout flow, no pre-built onboarding for someone who just hands over a credit card.
7. **14 niches × 3 tiers = 42 SKUs of implicit work.** The landing pages exist, but the offer behind them is identical. There's no niche-specific value claim baked into the package.

## 2. Recommended 3-Tier Structure: Starter / Growth / Scale

Repackaging principles applied:
- **Each tier solves one job.** Starter = visibility. Growth = pipeline. Scale = revenue ops.
- **Async-only is the product, not a limitation.** Branded as "WhatsApp-native CMO."
- **Every tier has a 30-day visible deliverable** so the buyer feels something ship.
- **Niches drive landing page copy and onboarding templates, not SKU sprawl.**

### Starter — "Get found by AI" — $249/mo, no setup

**Job-to-be-done:** "I'm invisible in ChatGPT and Google. Make me show up."

**What's included:**
- AEO audit + 12-month citation roadmap (delivered week 1)
- 4 AEO-optimized articles per month (published, not drafted)
- Schema + entity markup on existing site (one-time, included)
- Monthly citation report — actual ChatGPT/Perplexity/Claude pulls showing where you're cited
- NWM CRM Starter ($49 value) bundled
- WhatsApp async support, 24h response SLA
- Self-serve checkout with Stripe; productized onboarding video + intake form

**What's explicitly NOT included:**
- Paid ads (point them to Growth)
- Outbound / SDR (point them to Growth)
- Strategy calls (point them to Growth — async only at this tier)
- Custom content beyond AEO articles

**Success metric for the buyer:** First documented LLM citation within 60 days. If not delivered, next month free.

**Success metric for NWM:** 30%+ upgrade-to-Growth within 6 months. Current Lite retention is 48% at 12mo — we don't optimize Starter for tenure, we optimize for upgrade velocity.

**Setup fee:** $0 (preserve the wedge).

### Growth — "Build the pipeline" — $999/mo + ad mgmt — $499 setup

**Job-to-be-done:** "I have visibility. Now I need leads showing up in my inbox every week."

**What's included:**
- Everything in Starter
- **Pick one growth engine:** (a) Paid ads management (Google + Meta, $5k–$20k spend, billed at cost + 12%, min $300), OR (b) AI SDR outbound (3,000 personalized touches/mo, video intros, reply handling). One only — second engine is a $499/mo add-on.
- Email nurture: 3 sequences built in month 1, monitored ongoing
- Social media: 3 posts/wk on the buyer's primary platform (one channel only — buyer picks)
- Attribution dashboard — pipeline by source, weekly auto-refresh
- Bi-weekly async strategy memo (Loom + written)
- NWM CRM Pro ($249 value) bundled
- WhatsApp async, 8-hour response SLA on weekdays

**What's NOT included:**
- Video production (Scale)
- Voice AI / custom agents (Scale)
- Multi-channel social (one channel only — multi is Scale)
- Live calls (still async-first; one optional 30-min onboarding call as a courtesy)

**Success metric for the buyer:** Documented pipeline value at 90 days ≥ 6x retainer (i.e., $54k+ pipeline by day 90 on a $999 spend).

**Success metric for NWM:** 50% of Growth clients running both engines within 9 months (= $499 ARPU lift). Net retention 105%+.

**Why split the engines:** Growth currently bundles ads + SDR + social + email. Most clients use one of those seriously. Forcing the choice raises perceived focus, lowers delivery cost, and creates a clean expansion path *inside* the tier.

### Scale — "Run my revenue" — $2,499/mo + ad mgmt — $999 setup

**Job-to-be-done:** "I want one team accountable for the full marketing-to-revenue motion. I have $5M+ ARR or I'm pushing toward it."

**What's included:**
- Everything in Growth — both engines on (paid ads + AI SDR)
- Video Factory: 12 short-form assets/mo (Reels/Shorts/TikTok)
- Voice AI receptionist + qualification agent (configured + maintained)
- Custom workflow build: 2 automations/quarter spec'd to the buyer's process
- Demand-gen campaigns: 1 named launch per quarter (webinar, report, or interactive tool)
- Quarterly OKR planning (async doc + 60-min recorded review)
- Weekly written exec memo every Monday
- NWM CRM Agency ($449 value) bundled — supports multi-brand/franchise buyers
- Dedicated WhatsApp channel with Carlos for strategic escalations
- Priority on roadmap requests (input into platform direction)

**What's NOT included:**
- Live ongoing weekly calls (replace with the weekly written memo + on-demand voice notes — this is the async-native promise)
- Bespoke creative beyond Video Factory output
- Hiring/managing the buyer's internal team (different product — see Gap #3)

**Success metric for the buyer:** Quarterly attributed revenue ≥ 12x quarterly retainer (i.e., $90k+/qtr at the $7,497 base).

**Success metric for NWM:** 90%+ logo retention. NRR 115%+. 50% of Scale clients run an annual contract.

**Setup fee:** $999 (preserve as a commitment device — the weed-out works).

### Pricing summary (recommended)

| Tier | Monthly | Setup | Annual (15% off) | Target buyer ACV |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Starter | $249 | $0 | $2,540 | $2.5k–$3k |
| Growth | $999 + ad mgmt | $499 | $10,190 + ad mgmt | $14k–$22k |
| Scale | $2,499 + ad mgmt | $999 | $25,490 + ad mgmt | $34k–$52k |

Hold the price points. The repricing on 2026-04-21 is fresh; do not move the headline numbers again. The change is *what's inside* the tiers and *how they're sold*.

## 3. Niche Prioritization by Tier

The 14 niches are not equal buyers. Map them to where they convert, not where the landing page exists.

### Starter ($249) — niches that buy on Stripe checkout

These are price-sensitive, high-volume, low-trust-required SMBs. Self-serve flow.

1. **Beauty** (salons, spas, barbers) — high-volume SMB, $50k–$500k revenue, founder-operator
2. **Restaurants** — same shape; low ACV, but volume + referral density
3. **Local specialist / SMB (general)** — wedge for everything that doesn't fit a vertical
4. **Real estate** (solo agents) — self-serve fits; teams roll up to Growth

**Prioritize Starter acquisition spend on these four.** They map to the "47% of buyers ask AI before searching" thesis — these niches *desperately* want to show up in ChatGPT and have no in-house capacity.

### Growth ($999) — niches that need pipeline + a sales motion

5. **Health** (dental, vet, med-spa, clinics) — high-LTV patients, ad-driven, regulated; strong fit for paid ads engine
6. **Home services** (HVAC, plumbing, roofing, contractors) — paid-ads-native; LSAs + Google/Meta
7. **Automotive** (dealers, repair, detailing) — ad-driven, geo-bounded, urgent buyer intent
8. **Events / weddings** — long sales cycles, nurture-heavy, SDR engine fits
9. **Tourism** (hotels, tour operators, vacation rentals) — bilingual EN↔ES is a real differentiator here

**Prioritize Growth acquisition on health, home services, and tourism** — these have the budget and the existing-marketing-doesn't-work pain.

### Scale ($2,499) — niches with $5M+ revenue and complex revenue ops

10. **Law firms** — high-ACV cases, content-heavy, AEO + SDR + voice AI all stack
11. **Financial services** (RIAs, accountants, insurance, mortgage) — compliance-bound, content-heavy, long cycles
12. **Education** (private schools, tutoring chains, course creators) — multi-stakeholder buying, brand-heavy
13. **Wine / agriculture** — premium positioning, story-driven, often multi-brand (vineyard + tasting room + DTC)

**Prioritize Scale outbound on law firms and financial services.** These are the niches where the human CMO comparable ($8k–$18k/mo from Chief Outsiders) is the honest competitor — and our $2,499 looks like an obvious win.

### Niche I would deprioritize at the SKU level

**Education** is a tough self-serve buy — long sales cycles, committee buying, lots of customization. Either commit a Scale variant with a longer onboarding ramp, or treat it as project work (one-time AEO migration + CRM build) and skip the retainer pitch.

## 4. Product Gaps to Build Next

Ranked by RICE (rough), focused on what unlocks the Lever A volume + Scale upsell story.

### Gap 1: Self-serve Starter checkout + onboarding (RICE: high)

**Problem:** Starter at $249 needs to convert without a sales call, or the unit economics don't work (CAC must stay under $600). The current `contact.html?plan=cmo-lite` is a sales handoff.

**Build:**
- Stripe-hosted checkout for Starter only
- Productized intake form (15 questions, 8 minutes)
- Auto-provisioned CRM Starter account on payment
- 7-email onboarding sequence (already on the May execution plan — Task 13)
- AEO audit auto-runs in week 1, delivered as PDF + Loom on day 7

**Effort:** ~3 weeks engineering + content. Reuses existing CRM, intake forms, and audit tooling.

**Success metric:** 60% of Starter signups complete onboarding without human touch. CAC on Starter under $400.

**Build/buy/partner:** Build. We own the CRM and intake; outsourcing this defeats the margin model.

### Gap 2: Niche-specific onboarding templates (RICE: high)

**Problem:** 39 landing pages drive niche-specific traffic into a generic onboarding. The promise on the LP ("AI marketing for restaurants") evaporates on day 1.

**Build:**
- 14 niche playbooks living in the CRM as starting templates: pre-built ICP, 30 AEO query targets, 5 ad creative angles, 3 email sequences
- Onboarding form branches by niche
- Day-7 strategy doc auto-populates from the niche template

**Effort:** 1 week per niche (content-strategist agent), parallelizable. ~6 weeks for the 14 priority niches.

**Success metric:** Time-to-first-AEO-citation drops from 60 days to 30 days. Demo-to-close rate +25% on niche-targeted traffic.

**Build/buy/partner:** Build. This *is* our defensible IP.

### Gap 3: "Fractional CMO Embedded" — Scale+ tier above $2,499

**Problem:** We're capped at $2,499 + ad mgmt. The marketing plan says traditional fCMOs charge $8k–$18k. There is space between Scale and Chief Outsiders that we're leaving on the table for buyers with $10M+ revenue.

**Build (test, don't ship yet):** A $4,999/mo tier that adds:
- Hiring + managing the buyer's internal marketer (1 FTE)
- Quarterly board-deck-grade reporting
- Vendor management (the buyer's other agencies/tools)
- A named NWM strategist + Carlos on quarterly review

**Effort:** Pilot with 3 design-partner clients. Don't list publicly until M9.

**Success metric:** 3 pilot conversions at $4,999, 90%+ retention at 6 months → publicly list as "CMO Embedded."

**Build/buy/partner:** Partner first (recruit a fractional-CMO veteran as 1099 to lead the human side), then productize.

### Gap 4: Add-on marketplace inside Growth (RICE: medium-high)

**Problem:** Growth forces buyers to pick one engine (ads OR SDR). 50% will want both eventually. Today there's no clean expansion SKU.

**Build:** A flat $499/mo "second engine" add-on, plus discrete add-ons:
- Second social channel: $199/mo
- Extra 4 AEO articles/mo: $299/mo
- Email-only campaign launch: $799 one-time
- Bilingual content (EN+ES): +20% on tier price

**Effort:** Mostly billing + line-item plumbing in the CRM. ~1 week.

**Success metric:** ARPU on Growth lifts from $999 to $1,250 average within 6 months.

**Build/buy/partner:** Build.

### Gap 5: Annual pre-pay flow (RICE: medium)

**Problem:** 15% annual discount is announced on the pricing page. There is no checkout flow, no invoice template, no auto-renew logic.

**Build:**
- Stripe annual subscription product per tier
- Renewal-60-days-out email sequence
- "Switch to annual" upsell email at month 4 to monthly Growth/Scale clients

**Effort:** 1 week.

**Success metric:** 25% of Growth + Scale clients on annual by M12. (Annual customers retain ~30% better — this directly defends the kill-switch threshold of 85% retention.)

**Build/buy/partner:** Build.

### Gap 6: Kill the Lite "monthly async strategy note"

**Problem:** It's a deliverable that creates obligation but signals nothing the buyer values. Lite churns at 11 months because nothing felt like it was happening on month 8.

**Action:** Replace with a *monthly citation report* (auto-generated, real ChatGPT/Perplexity pulls, screenshots). Higher perceived value, lower delivery cost, ties directly to the AEO-pillar promise.

**Effort:** 2 weeks — a script that runs the buyer's brand against the LLMs and renders a PDF.

**Success metric:** Lite month-12 retention from 48% to 55%, OR upgrade-to-Growth from current ~unknown to 30%.

## 5. Non-goals (what we are NOT doing)

- We are **not** moving headline prices. The 2026-04-21 reset is fresh.
- We are **not** adding a 4th public tier this quarter. Embedded CMO is a pilot only.
- We are **not** offering scheduled live calls as a default. Async-first is the product. One optional onboarding call at Growth/Scale is the ceiling.
- We are **not** building niche-specific SKUs. 14 niches × 3 tiers stays at 3 SKUs with niche-specific *templates* underneath.
- We are **not** continuing to bundle the SDR engine into Growth at the $999 price. That is the single biggest unbundling change in this proposal.
- We are **not** adding HubSpot/Salesforce integrations as a sales talking point. Bundled NWM CRM is the answer.

## 6. Open questions for CEO

1. **Approve unbundling Growth into "pick one engine + add-on"?** This is the single biggest packaging change. It risks pushback from existing Growth clients who feel they're losing scope. Recommend grandfathering existing accounts at current scope for 12 months.
2. **Approve killing the monthly async strategy note on Starter** in favor of the auto-generated citation report? (Net: lower delivery cost, higher perceived value.)
3. **Approve Embedded CMO ($4,999) pilot at M6?** Requires recruiting 1099 fractional-CMO talent — first time we put a non-Carlos human on a deliverable.
4. **14 niches confirmed — but Education looks weak as a retainer fit.** Move to project-only, or commit a Scale variant?
5. **Self-serve Starter checkout — Stripe only, or also Mercado Pago at launch?** Adds 2 weeks but covers 30% LATAM ICP.
