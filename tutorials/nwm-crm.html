0. Before you start — pre-flight checklist

Do these before you create an account. 80% of bad CRM rollouts skip this step, which is why 80% of CRM rollouts fail.

Pre-flight (30 min, get it right once)

  • Export your current contact list (CSV) — even if it's a mess, export it
  • Decide who owns the CRM internally (one person, not a committee)
  • List the 5–7 pipeline stages your sales actually goes through today
  • List every source of inbound leads (website forms, referrals, events, paid ads, etc.)
  • Decide on 3–5 custom fields you truly need (industry, deal size, use case, referral source, churn reason)
  • Pick a naming convention for deals (e.g. {Company} — {Service} — {Start Month})
  • Get SMTP credentials ready (Gmail App Password, Outlook, or SendGrid API key)
  • Block 4 hours on your calendar for setup — don't try to squeeze it between meetings

The #1 decision you must make upfront

Contacts vs. companies: which is the primary record?

  • If you sell to individuals (coaches, therapists, real estate agents) → contact-primary. Deals attach to contacts directly.
  • If you sell to businesses (B2B SaaS, agencies, professional services) → company-primary. Deals attach to companies; contacts are attached to companies.

You can change this later, but migrating mid-stream is painful. Decide now.

⚠️ The most common first-week mistake

Importing 10,000 contacts before configuring pipeline stages. You end up with 10,000 contacts in "New Lead" with no structure. Fix the pipeline first, then import.

1. What NWM CRM is (and what it isn't)

NWM CRM is the operational hub for sales and customer relationships at SMBs. It replaces 4–7 tools for most customers:

  • HubSpot / Pipedrive / Salesforce (CRM core)
  • Mailchimp / Klaviyo / ActiveCampaign (marketing email)
  • Calendly / Cal.com (booking, via integration)
  • Intercom / Drift (basic support messaging)
  • Google Sheets (the list you've been maintaining manually — stop)

What it's good at

  • Single source of truth — every email, call, meeting, and deal in one contact record
  • Speed — built on vanilla PHP + MySQL + vanilla JS. Pages load in < 400 ms. HubSpot takes 2–4 seconds.
  • AI-assistance — draft replies, score leads, summarize calls — all on tap
  • Price — $49/mo starter, $99 pro, $249 enterprise. HubSpot Professional is $800/mo for the same features.

What it's not

  • Not for > 500-person sales orgs — no multi-division reporting, no territory management. Stay on Salesforce if you're that big.
  • Not a service desk — we do basic ticketing, but for full CS, pair with Zendesk or Intercom
  • Not a marketing automation monster — we do email + SMS + WhatsApp well. For ads spend attribution and multi-touch models, pair with HubSpot Marketing Hub or Ortto
  • Not a payments processor — we link to Stripe, we don't replace it

4. Data model — contacts · companies · deals · activities

Understanding the data model is the single highest-leverage thing you can do. It determines how everything else works.

4.1 Contacts

A contact is a person — named human with an email or phone. Core fields:

nameRequired. Full display name. We index this for search. emailUnique per account. Primary identifier. Lowercase-stored. phoneE.164 format recommended (+14155551234). We normalize on save. companyFree-text OR foreign key to companies table (see 4.2). role / title"VP Marketing", "Founder", "Procurement Lead" — used in reporting + personalization. statusenum: lead · prospect · customer · churned. Auto-updates when deals close. valueLifetime value in your account currency. Auto-calculated from closed deals. last_contactDate of last logged activity. Key metric for stale-contact reports. tagsFree-form labels. Use for segmentation ("conference-RSA-2026", "enterprise-fit", "passed-budget-check"). sourceWhere they came from. Pre-loaded options: website-form, referral, ad, event, cold-outbound, other. ownerThe team member who owns this relationship. Affects permissions + reporting.

4.2 Companies

A company is an organization. Contacts attach to companies. Deals can attach to either.

nameRequired. Canonical name — we dedupe on lowercase + no-whitespace match. domainThe root domain (acme.com). Used for auto-attachment of contacts whose email matches. industryFree-text with autocomplete from your existing industries. sizeenum: 1-10 · 11-50 · 51-200 · 201-500 · 501-1000 · 1000+ arr / revenueAnnual revenue of the company. Optional but powerful for ICP scoring. country / cityFor geo reporting + timezone-aware outreach.

Auto-attachment rule: when you add a contact with email sarah@acme.com, we check for a company with domain acme.com. If it exists, we attach. If not, we create it with name Acme (inferred) — you can rename later.

4.3 Deals

A deal is a specific sales opportunity. One company can have many deals (new business, renewal, upsell).

Drives probability + aging color. valueTotal deal value. For subscriptions, use annual value (ARR). probability0–100%. Can be stage-default or manually overridden. expected_closeDate. Drives forecast reports. days_in_stageAuto-calculated. Resets when you move to a new stage. sourceInherits from contact source by default. Override if the specific deal has a different origin.

4.5 Custom fields — when and how

Settings → Custom Fields. You can add custom fields to contacts, companies, or deals. Field types:

  • Text (single-line, multi-line)
  • Number (integer, decimal, currency)
  • Date / Datetime
  • Select (single dropdown)
  • Multi-select (checkboxes)
  • URL, Email, Phone (typed text with validation)
  • Boolean (yes/no toggle)
⚠️ Don't go custom-field-crazy

5 custom fields is a lot. 20 is unmanageable. Every custom field is a field your team must fill in, or it's just noise. Start with 3, add more only when you miss them.

Our recommended starter custom fields for B2B:

  1. use_case (select) — what they want to solve
  2. budget_range (select: <$5k, $5-20k, $20-50k, $50k+)
  3. decision_timeframe (select: this week, this month, this quarter, next quarter, no timeline)

5. Importing data — CSV, HubSpot, Pipedrive, Salesforce

5.1 CSV import — the canonical method

1

Prepare the CSV

Required columns: name, email. Recommended: phone, company, role, status, source, tags, owner_email.

Format rules:

  • UTF-8 encoding. Excel exports as Windows-1252 by default → re-save as "CSV UTF-8"
  • Comma-separated. Quote fields that contain commas ("Smith, John")
  • One header row, then data rows
  • Phone numbers in E.164 format recommended (+14155551234)
  • Dates in ISO format (2026-04-20)
  • Tags comma-separated inside a single cell: "vip,enterprise,rsa-2026"

Download our template CSV →

2

Upload

Settings → Import Data → Contacts → Upload CSV. File size limit: 10 MB per upload (~30,000 rows). For larger, split into multiple files.

3

Map columns

You see a 3-row preview of your data. For each source column, pick the target field. Our importer auto-detects common column names (email → email, "Full Name" → name, etc.); you fix the rest.

Map custom fields: If your CSV has a column we don't recognize (e.g. "Lead Source Detail"), map it to "Create new custom field" → give it a name + type.

4

Dedup strategy

Choose what to do when a CSV row matches an existing contact (by email):

  • Skip (safe default) — keep existing data, ignore CSV row
  • Overwrite — CSV wins, replaces existing data
  • Merge (non-empty wins) — CSV fills in blanks, preserves existing non-blank fields

Use "Merge" for monthly re-imports. Use "Skip" for one-off imports. Use "Overwrite" only if you're sure the CSV is more recent than what's in the CRM.

5

Preview & confirm

Shows: X new contacts, Y updates, Z skips (duplicates or invalid). Any row with a problem (missing email, invalid date) shows with a red flag. You can download the error CSV, fix, and re-upload the fixes.

6

Run the import

Click "Import". Progress bar shows live count. ~1000 rows/sec. 10k rows completes in ~10 seconds. You get an email when done with a summary.

⚠️ Import validation pitfalls

(1) Rows with no email get stored only if you also map a phone (at least one identifier required). (2) Bounced-looking emails (e.g. noreply@, donotreply@) get imported but auto-tagged suspicious-email. Review + clean before campaigns. (3) Dates in American MM/DD/YYYY format without the year century (e.g. 04/20/26) are ambiguous — always use 2026-04-20.

5.2 HubSpot migration

Settings → Integrations → HubSpot → "Migrate from HubSpot". You'll need:

  1. HubSpot Private App Token (Settings → Integrations → Private Apps in HubSpot)
  2. Scopes: crm.objects.contacts.read, crm.objects.companies.read, crm.objects.deals.read, crm.objects.owners.read

Paste the token. We fetch contacts, companies, deals, and activities. A typical 5k-contact HubSpot account migrates in 15–30 minutes. We preserve:

  • Contact + company + deal records
  • Associations (which contacts belong to which companies + deals)
  • Owner assignments (mapped to NWM CRM users by email — users must already exist)
  • Activity timeline (emails, calls, meetings, notes — mapped to closest equivalent type)
  • Deal stages (mapped to your NWM CRM pipeline — you choose the mapping)

What we don't bring across:

  • Workflows (rebuild in NWM Automation — usually 30–60 min each)
  • Custom objects (HubSpot custom objects don't have a 1:1 equivalent — we import them as tags)
  • Marketing email history (kept in HubSpot, but attribution carries via "first-touch source" field)

5.3 Pipedrive migration

Same flow. Needs Pipedrive API token (Settings → Personal Preferences → API). Migrates persons, organizations, deals, activities.

5.4 Salesforce migration

Enterprise plan only (Salesforce export is complex). We do it as a white-glove service: you give us read-only API access, we extract + transform + load, validate with you, and hand over within 3 business days. Included free with Enterprise plan.

6. Pipeline design — stages, probability, aging

6.1 Designing your stages

Good pipelines have 5–7 stages. Too few: not actionable. Too many: data entry hell. Here's the default we ship, battle-tested across 500+ customers:

StageMeaningDefault probabilityAging threshold
1. New LeadJust entered CRM, not yet contacted5%2 days (if > 2, overdue)
2. ContactedFirst outreach made, awaiting response15%5 days
3. QualifiedResponded + fits ICP + has budget/authority/need/timeline (BANT)30%10 days
4. Proposal SentFormal proposal / contract delivered55%7 days
5. NegotiationIn back-and-forth on terms / redlines / pricing75%10 days
6. Closed WonContract signed, first payment made (or invoice accepted)100%—
7. Closed LostDeal won't close (pick reason from list)0%—

6.2 Customizing stages

1

Open stage editor

Settings → Pipelines → (pick pipeline) → Stages. Drag to reorder. Click to rename, change color, set default probability.

2

Set aging thresholds

Per stage: "days before yellow warning" and "days before red alert". Defaults in table above.

3

Lock certain stages

You can mark stages as "closed-won" or "closed-lost" (any number of each). When a deal moves into a closed-lost stage, it must pick a reason from your reasons list (configured per pipeline).

6.3 Multi-pipeline setups

On Professional + Enterprise plans, you can have multiple pipelines. Common setups:

  • New Business — as above, for net-new deals
  • Renewals — 3-4 stages: Renewal Due → Renewal At-Risk → Renewed → Churned
  • Upsell / Cross-sell — same structure as new business but triggered from customer status
  • Partner Deals — leads from partners, different commission rules

Each deal lives in exactly one pipeline. Moving a deal between pipelines requires permission (Enterprise plan feature).

6.4 Deal aging — how to actually use it

The pipeline view color-codes cards:

  • White — under threshold, healthy
  • Yellow — between threshold and 2× threshold, getting stale
  • Red — > 2× threshold, likely dead

Weekly Monday ritual:

  1. Open pipeline view
  2. Filter to "red" cards
  3. For each: one of three actions — (a) log a specific next step, (b) move to a different stage, (c) move to Closed-Lost with a reason

If you do this every Monday for 10 weeks, your forecast accuracy improves 40–60%. Stale deals kill pipelines silently.

8.3 Moving deals through stages

Three ways to advance a deal:

  1. Drag and drop in pipeline view (fastest)
  2. Change stage dropdown on deal detail page
  3. Programmatic — via automation or API (see section 14)

What happens when a deal moves stages:

  • Activity logged: stage_change with from/to + timestamp
  • days_in_stage resets to 0
  • Probability auto-updates to new stage's default (unless manually overridden)
  • Automations trigger (e.g. "when deal → Proposal Sent, create task: follow up in 3 days")
  • Notifications fire based on your team's preferences

8.4 Closing a deal

Closing Won:

  1. Drag to Closed Won (or update stage)
  2. Modal asks: Final value? (sometimes differs from expected), Close reason? (pick "Sold - primary driver" from list), Close date? (defaults today)
  3. Contact status auto-updates: prospect → customer
  4. Revenue rolls up to dashboards immediately
  5. If Customer Success handoff is configured: CS rep auto-assigned, onboarding automation fires

Closing Lost:

  1. Drag to Closed Lost
  2. Modal asks: Reason (picked from configured list — e.g. "Price too high", "Went with competitor", "No timeline", "No budget this year")
  3. Optional: competitor name, if "Went with competitor"
  4. Optional: nurture? (add to "Closed Lost - Nurture" sequence for 90-day re-engagement)

8.5 Re-opening a closed deal

Situations happen. Open the deal → "Re-open" button. Pick a new stage. Original close log preserved in timeline. The deal returns to the active pipeline.

8.6 Deal attachments & collaboration

On the deal detail page:

  • Files tab — upload proposals, contracts, competitive research. Versioning: v1, v2, v3 (auto-numbered on re-upload).
  • @-mentions — in any note or comment, type @ + teammate name. They get notified.
  • Internal comments — team-only discussion, never visible to the contact.
  • Documents integration — generate proposal from template, e-sign, track opens (see section 10).

9. Conversations inbox — email, SMS, WhatsApp setup

9.1 Connecting email (the most important thing you'll do)

Settings → Integrations → Email. Three options:

Option 1: Google Workspace / Gmail

  1. Click "Connect Google"
  2. OAuth consent screen → grant scopes: Gmail read, Gmail send, Gmail modify
  3. Pick the account to connect
  4. Done. Setup time: 60 seconds.

Note: if your Google Workspace admin disabled 3rd-party apps, you'll get "permission denied". Ask them to whitelist NWM CRM (OAuth client ID shown on the error page).

Option 2: Microsoft 365 / Outlook

  1. Click "Connect Microsoft"
  2. Azure AD consent → scopes: Mail.Read, Mail.Send, Mail.ReadWrite
  3. Done.

Option 3: SMTP (any provider)

For self-hosted email, SendGrid, Mailgun, Amazon SES, or anyone not covered above.

SMTP hoste.g. smtp.sendgrid.net, smtp.mailgun.org, email-smtp.us-east-1.amazonaws.com Port465 (SSL) or 587 (TLS). 587 preferred. UsernameSMTP user (for SendGrid: literally "apikey") PasswordSMTP password / API key From addressThe email address emails will send from From namee.g. "Carlos at NetWebMedia" Reply-toWhere replies go. Often same as From, but can differ.

We run a test send immediately. If it works, you're connected. If not, we show the exact error (most commonly: auth failed, sending limit hit, or DNS records missing).

DNS records — non-negotiable for deliverability

Regardless of which option you picked, set these on your sending domain:

  • SPF — authorizes your sender. Settings → Email → "Show SPF" shows your exact record. Copy to your DNS as a TXT record at the root (@).
  • DKIM — signs outbound messages. We generate a keypair. Copy the provided TXT record into DNS (usually at nwmcrm._domainkey).
  • DMARC — policy. Start with v=DMARC1; p=none; rua=mailto:dmarc@yourdomain.com. Monitor 30 days, then move to p=quarantine, then p=reject.
⚠️ Don't send marketing email without DNS set up

You'll land in spam. Google + Microsoft both require authenticated email starting Feb 2024. Skip DNS = skip the inbox.

11.5 Suppression & unsubscribes (critical)

Every marketing email automatically includes an unsubscribe link (non-removable). When clicked:

  1. Contact marked unsubscribed: true
  2. Removed from all active sequences
  3. Future campaigns automatically skip them
  4. Re-subscription requires them to re-opt-in via a form
⚠️ Never try to bypass unsubscribes

Legally, you must honor every unsubscribe within 10 business days (CAN-SPAM) or immediately (GDPR/CASL). We enforce this at the platform level — the suppression list is not editable. If you need to contact someone for a non-commercial reason (e.g. renewal), use transactional 1:1 email, not marketing send.

15. Troubleshooting — the 20 issues we see most

  1. "My emails go to spam" — 9 times out of 10 it's missing SPF/DKIM/DMARC. Settings → Email → Run Deliverability Check. Fix the red flags.
  2. "I imported a CSV but contacts are missing" — check import log (Settings → Import History). Rows without email + phone are skipped. Re-upload fixed rows.
  3. "HubSpot sync stopped" — token likely expired or scope removed. Settings → Integrations → HubSpot → Reconnect.
  4. "Deal won't move to next stage" — check if stage requires a field to be filled (e.g. "expected_close date required to leave Qualified"). Error appears in red at bottom of modal.
  5. "Automation didn't fire" — Automation → (sequence) → Log shows why. Common reasons: contact already enrolled, filter didn't match, trigger disabled.
  6. "Campaign open rate is 0%" — most likely your image host blocked (Gmail image proxy caches). Open tracking relies on image loads. Still use click rate as the real signal.
  7. "I can't find a contact I just created" — 98% of the time it's a filter applied to your contacts view. Click "Clear filters".
  8. "Calendar sync is pulling old events" — initial sync pulls 90 days back. If you need longer history, Settings → Integrations → Calendar → Extend sync range.
  9. "SMS won't send" — Twilio balance might be $0, or A2P 10DLC registration pending. Twilio console shows the real error.
  10. "My custom field isn't showing" — you added it to Contacts but are viewing it on a Company or Deal. Custom fields are per-object.
  11. "Duplicate contacts keep appearing" — check import settings. Switch to "Merge" not "Create new" for recurring imports.
  12. "I'm getting rate-limited on the API" — default is 1000 req/min. Professional gets 5000, Enterprise custom. Batch writes into 100-item chunks.
  13. "Pipeline is slow to load" — you have > 500 deals in active stages. Archive closed-won/lost older than 6 months: Settings → Data → Archive Old Deals.
  14. "Reports show wrong numbers" — timezone mismatch. Every user has their own timezone; reports aggregate in the account's timezone. Settings → General → Timezone.
  15. "Form submissions aren't coming through" — check the form's "Status" is Published, not Draft. And that the target field names match your current schema.
  16. "@-mentions don't notify" — teammate has notifications off. Settings → Notifications → review.
  17. "I can't log in" — magic link expired (5-min window). Request a new one. If blocked by corporate email filter, try the password fallback (Settings → Security).
  18. "I lost access after an SSO change" — admin needs to re-sync your account. Contact support if you're the only admin.
  19. "Export CSV is truncated" — at 100k rows, we split into multiple CSVs (downloaded as a ZIP).
  20. "Whatsapp template was rejected" — Meta template rejections usually due to marketing content in a Utility template, or vice versa. Re-submit with correct category.
