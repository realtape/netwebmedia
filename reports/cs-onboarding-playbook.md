# NetWebMedia Client Onboarding Playbook
## Day 0 – Month 1 (Kickoff through 30-Day Delivery)

**Version:** 1.0  
**Owner:** Customer Success Manager  
**Last updated:** April 28, 2026  
**Applies to:** All fCMO retainer tiers (Lite, Growth, Scale)

---

## Overview

First 90 days decide the next 900. This playbook ensures every client hits critical milestones — discovery, asset delivery, quick wins — while being supported entirely through async channels (WhatsApp, email). No mandatory Zoom calls. Value is proven in writing.

**Philosophy:** Outcome reviews, not activity reviews. The client should hear "here's what changed for you" by Day 30, not "here's what we did."

---

### 1.1 CRM Intake (Hour 0)

**Action:**
- Create client record in crm-vanilla with:
  - Legal name, primary contact email, phone
  - Company website, social profiles
  - Deal details: tier (Lite / Growth / Scale), MSA signed date, start date, MRR, ad budget (if Growth/Scale)
  - Industry niche (from 14 list: law_firms, restaurants, health, beauty, smb, real_estate, local_specialist, automotive, education, events_weddings, financial_services, home_services, wine_agriculture, tourism)

- Set internal reminders: Week 1 check-in, Month 1 review, Month 2 renewal signal check

**Why:** Single source of truth. Prevents communication gaps between Project Manager, Content Strategist, and Engineering Lead. If anything goes wrong, the audit trail is in one place.

### 2.1 Day 1–2: Discovery Closes

**Owner:** Project Manager + Content Strategist  
**Input:** Client's completed intake brief  
**Output:** Positioning doc (draft)

**Actions:**

1. **Competitive audit** (internal, Content Strategist):
   - Visit 3 competitor websites listed by client
   - Note their headline, value prop, proof points, pricing
   - Identify positioning gaps (where they're weak, where we can be strong)
   - 15-min summary: "[Client] can own the [positioning angle] that [Competitor 1] ignores"

2. **Positioning workshop** (async document):
   - Create 1-page doc with:
     - **Current positioning (if exists):** Client's own headline from their site
     - **Competitive context:** What the 3 competitors own
     - **Proposed positioning:** [Company name] is the [category] that [key differentiator] for [ICP]
     - **3 proof angles** (case studies, data, proof of concept we'll build)
   - Send to client, ask: "Does this ring true? Anything to shift?"

3. **Brand intake form** (for Growth/Scale):
   - Logo, brand colors, fonts (client-provided)
   - Tone of voice: formal vs casual, CEO-forward vs team-forward
   - 3 words that describe the brand
   - 1 brand story (origin, why you exist)

**Checkpoint:** Positioning approved or revised by client EOD Day 2.

## Part 4: Health Score Definition

### How We Know the Client Is Happy (and Will Renew)

**Owner:** CSM (tracked weekly)  
**Cadence:** Weekly spot-check, monthly rollup

---

### 4.1 Health Score Model (0–100)

**Components:**

| Signal | Weight | Good (green) | Caution (yellow) | Risk (red) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Usage** (logins, actions in CRM) | 25% | >3 logins/week | 1–2 logins/week | 0 logins/week for 2 weeks |
| **Sentiment** (email tone, responsiveness) | 25% | Fast replies, enthusiastic language | Slower replies, neutral tone | No replies for >5 days, negative language |
| **Outcomes** (leads, conversions, revenue) | 30% | Hitting 80%+ of Day 30 goals | On track (50–79%) | Below 50% of goals |
| **Stakeholder engagement** (# of team members using platform) | 20% | 3+ people actively involved | 1–2 people | Single contact only, no expansion |

**Scoring:**

- **80–100:** Renew likely. Upsell conversation okay.
- **60–79:** Renew likely with intervention (monthly QBR, ask for feedback).
- **40–59:** At-risk. Needs save play.
- **0–39:** Churn likely. Offboarding conversation or intensive save.

## Part 5: Renewal Trigger Signals (Day 60)

### What to Watch 60 Days Out

**Owner:** CSM (forecast by Day 45)  
**Cadence:** Monthly check → escalate at Day 60

---

### 5.1 Renewal Green Flags (Client Will Renew)

✓ Client hits 70%+ of Day 30 goals  
✓ 3+ team members using the platform actively  
✓ Client proactively shared a customer success story or referral  
✓ Client approved feature request or expansion plan  
✓ Client replies to check-ins in <24 hours  
✓ "This is working" language in emails (vs. "Let's see how this goes")  
✓ Client attends or requests optional strategy calls  

---

### 5.2 Renewal Red Flags (Client May Churn)

✗ Client goes silent for >7 days (no replies to emails/WhatsApp)  
✗ Outcomes are below 40% of Day 30 goals (but no conversation about fixing it)  
✗ Usage drops: <1 login/week for 2 consecutive weeks  
✗ Contact person changed but new person wasn't onboarded (org chaos)  
✗ Client references budget cuts or CFO scrutiny (without context)  
✗ Negative language: "Let's pause for a month" or "This isn't a priority right now"  
✗ Competitor mention (client evaluating alternative)  

---

---

### 5.4 Red Flag Escalation (Day 60+)

**If client is at risk (score <60):**

**Escalate to:** CSM → Operations Manager (internal save play orchestration)

**Save play trigger:** Client hasn't replied to Day 55 renewal email OR score dropped 20+ points since Month 1 end.

**Save play anatomy:**

1. **Root cause analysis** (internal, 30 min):
   - What metric dropped? (usage, outcomes, sentiment, engagement)
   - When did it drop? (between which two weeks)
   - Did anything change on our side? (slower delivery, missed deadline)
   - Did anything change on their side? (stakeholder left, budget cut, priority shift)

2. **Intervention** (within 2 business days):
   - **If usage issue:** Offer 1:1 onboarding call or whiteboard session
   - **If outcome issue:** Pivot strategy (increase spend, pause underperforming channel, shift to different audience)
   - **If sentiment issue:** Acknowledge any frustration, ask directly: "What would make this work for you?"
   - **If engagement issue:** Ask if the right stakeholder is involved; add them to communication

3. **Renewal decision timeline** (clear ask):
   - "Let's lock in a decision by [date]. If it's not working, let's talk about what would make it work OR what a wind-down looks like."
   - Avoid extending the conversation indefinitely.

### Escalation Paths

**If client is stuck on a deliverable (2+ days late):**
1. CSM reaches out via WhatsApp (same day)
2. Project Manager joins conversation if technical blocker
3. If still stuck by Day 2: Move to async workaround (prefilled template, client just reviews)

**If client doesn't respond to critical message (>3 business days):**
1. CSM tries one more time via WhatsApp
2. If still silent: Call during business hours (only if client has opted into calls)
3. If unreachable: Flag to Operations Manager (may indicate bigger problem)

**If client rejects deliverable or asks for major rework:**
1. CSM clarifies what's wrong in writing (avoid phone calls that reset context)
2. Document feedback in CRM (protects against repeat feedback)
3. Rework is 1x free, then becomes a change order (protects scope creep)

**If client churn is likely (score <40):**
1. CSM escalates to Operations Manager
2. Operations Manager + CSM run 1-hour save play strategy session
3. Offer: targeted intervention (pivot strategy, add feature, reduce scope) OR professional offboarding
4. Decision by [date]. Don't drag it out.

## Part 8: Success Definition (How We Know It Worked)

**A client has successful Month 1 onboarding if:**
