Frequently asked
Questions restaurants ask us
What schema markup does a restaurant need to appear in ChatGPT and AI Overviews? ▼

Start with: Restaurant (cuisine, price range, reservations), MenuSection (for menu categories), Dish (with ingredients and descriptions for signature items), AggregateRating (pulling your Google reviews), and LocalBusiness (address, phone, service areas). Restaurant + Dish schema is highest-ROI — AI engines match menu descriptions to user queries. Focus there first.

How long does AEO take to start driving reservations for a restaurant? ▼

First citations: 6-12 weeks after publishing AEO-optimized content with schema. Measurable reservation lift: 2-4 months. Compounding advantage: 6+ months. Early movers have massive edge — the longer your content has been in AI training data, the more consistent the recommendations. Restaurants that started in Q1 2026 are already seeing 2-3 AI recommendations per week.

Can a single-location restaurant compete with chains in AI search results? ▼

Yes. In fact, smaller restaurants have an advantage: lower competition in niche + location combinations. If you're a boutique Italian restaurant in Denver, you're competing with maybe 3-5 other similar restaurants for 'best Italian restaurant Denver' queries in AI. Chains face higher competition in national queries. AEO rewards specificity and locality — single-location restaurants with strong local content and specific cuisine/vibe descriptions win more often than generic chain listings.

Is Google Business Profile enough, or do restaurants need a separate AEO strategy? ▼

Google Business Profile is foundational but not sufficient. GBP drives 'restaurants near me' and casual neighborhood searches (80% of restaurant queries). AI Overviews drive high-intent, occasion-based searches (15%, fastest growing). A restaurant optimized for GBP only misses 40% of their ideal customer base — the ones asking 'where should I take someone special?' vs 'what's open nearby?' You need both.

What's the difference between AEO and local SEO for restaurants? ▼

Local SEO targets Google Maps and organic search for location-based queries: 'restaurants near me,' 'pizza [zip code].' AEO targets AI answer engines for experience-based queries: 'best date night restaurant,' 'farm-to-table dining.' They use different signals. Local SEO rewards: citations (NAP consistency), reviews, GBP optimization. AEO rewards: structured content (schema), clear descriptions of cuisine/vibe/occasion, E-E-A-T signals. Do local SEO first (it's faster and more ROI in year 1). Add AEO as the compounding channel (builds authority for year 2+).
