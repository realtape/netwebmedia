# Discovery Call Cheat Sheet · Law Firm + AEO Audit Pitch

> One-pager. Keep this open during the call. Goal: **buyer says yes to the $997 audit by end of call.**

---

## First 60 seconds — set the frame

> "Hey {firstName}, thanks for jumping on. Before we dig in, two things:
> First — this is a 15-min call. I'm not going to pitch you anything you didn't already ask about.
> Second — at the end I'll either send you the audit checkout link, or I won't. You tell me which.
> Sound good?"

Wait for their "yeah."

> "Cool. So — what made you reply to the message I sent Tuesday?"

**Shut up. Listen.** First answer is gold.

---

## The 3 pain points to listen for (law firms)

If you hear any of these, you have a buyer. Mirror back the pain in their words, then transition to the audit.

| Pain point | What it sounds like | Mirror back |
|---|---|---|
| **Intake latency** | "We're missing leads on weekends" / "Calls come in and nobody picks up" / "Our intake form is broken" | "Got it — so you're losing leads in the gap between inquiry and a real human picking up." |
| **AEO citation gaps** | "Nobody finds us online" / "I Googled our firm and we don't show up" / "ChatGPT doesn't know we exist" | "Got it — so when prospects ask ChatGPT or Google 'best {practiceArea} attorney in {city}', you're not in the answer." |
| **Referral nurture** | "Most of our cases come from referrals" / "I should send something to past clients" / "No system for staying in touch" | "Got it — so referrals are working but inconsistent because there's no system. You're at the mercy of who happens to remember you." |

---

## The pitch — 90 seconds, no fluff

After they describe their pain:

> "Here's what we do that fixes that specifically:
>
> We run a thing called an AEO Audit. $997, delivered in 48 hours. 18 pages.
>
> We take your firm's name, your practice areas, and the cities you serve. Run 15 prospect-style queries across ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, and Gemini — the actual answer engines real people use now.
>
> The output is two things:
> 1. Your AEO Citation Score — usually under 30 out of 100 — and the top-3 firm in your market for comparison. So you see the gap.
> 2. A 90-day action plan ranked by impact. Schema changes, content depth, location keywords, intake fixes. Specific. Not 'do better marketing.'
>
> The audit credits 100% toward any retainer you sign in 90 days. So if you decide later you want us running this for you, the $997 is essentially the deposit on the first month."

## Three objection handlers (top of mind)

### "I don't trust the AEO citation stuff yet"
> "Fair. Try this — open ChatGPT right now, type 'best {practiceArea} attorney in {city}'. Tell me if you're in the answer.
>
> [Wait — almost certainly no.]
>
> That's the data. Whether ChatGPT becomes a meaningful traffic source for law firms is a different question. The audit is $997 — if I'm wrong about AEO, you've spent $997 to confirm I'm wrong. If I'm right, you have a 60-day head start on every competitor."

### "$997 is a lot for an audit"
> "Compared to one missed referral? It's a 10-minute settlement.
>
> But here's the actual math — if you decide to run a CMO retainer with us later, the $997 credits 100% to your first month. So worst case, you spent $997 and got a 90-day roadmap. Best case, you spent it 60 days early on a retainer you'd be paying anyway."

### "We're already working with another agency"
> "Got it. Two questions:
> 1. Are they running AEO citation work? (Not SEO. AEO. They're different.)
> 2. Have they given you a written audit with a citation score?
>
> If yes to both — great, you're set. If no to either — that's exactly what the $997 audit fills in. Your existing agency keeps doing their thing. We just give you the AEO baseline."

## The close

You're going for one of two outcomes. Decide before the call which you want:

### Outcome A · Sell the audit on the call (best case)

> "Here's what I'd do. I'll send you the Stripe checkout link right now. $997. The audit ships Friday by 9am your time. I'll review it personally before it goes out.
>
> If after reading you want to talk about the retainer fit — great, we set up 30 min next week. If not, you have the 90-day plan in writing.
>
> Want the link?"

If yes → send Stripe Checkout link in the chat / via WhatsApp DURING the call. Strike while warm.

### Outcome B · "Let me think about it"

> "Totally fine. Two things for the road:
>
> 1. I'll send you the link tonight in case you change your mind in the next 48 hours.
> 2. If you're not ready, the audit credit stays valid for 90 days regardless. So 'no this week' isn't 'no forever' — just 'no right now.'
>
> Sound good?"

Then send the email follow-up template within 2 hours of the call.

---

## What to NOT do

- ❌ Don't quote CMO retainer pricing on this call. The audit is the only product on the table.
- ❌ Don't name any specific past law-firm client. Stay anonymous — "a personal-injury firm in the Midwest…" (durable rule per CLAUDE.md).
- ❌ Don't do a screenshare demo of the platform. The audit IS the demo.
- ❌ Don't say "fractional CMO" — most buyers don't know what that means. Say "marketing audit" or "AEO audit" only.
- ❌ Don't try to upsell to CMO Growth on this call. That's the *next* call, after the audit lands.

---

## Post-call within 5 minutes

Send this WhatsApp / email regardless of outcome:

> "Hey {firstName} — Carlos. Thanks for the 15 minutes.
>
> Per our chat: [audit link if Outcome A / "thinking it over" if B]
>
> Question for the record: what was the #1 thing you took away from our call?
>
> — Carlos"

The "what was the #1 takeaway" question is the highest-ROI follow-up question in B2B sales. People answer it. And their answer tells you exactly what to lead with in any future follow-up.

## Pre-call ritual (do this 10 min before)

1. Open the buyer's website. Memorize 1 practice area + 1 city.
2. Open ChatGPT. Type "best {practiceArea} attorney in {city}". Read the answer. See if their firm shows up. Note the actual #1 result for namedropping in the call.
3. Open `docs/sales-management-simulation/artifacts/proposal.html` in a tab — reference if needed for retainer talk, but DON'T pitch it on this call.
4. Have the Stripe Checkout link copied to clipboard. Or open `pricing.html` AEO Audit section ready to share screen.
5. Open `/crm-vanilla/contacts.html` filtered to the prospect's record. Note their email-open history, last touch.

15 min total. Hang up better prepared than 90% of fractional CMO calls ever conducted.
