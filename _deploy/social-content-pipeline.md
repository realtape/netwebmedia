### Thread 2: Law Firm Audit Findings
**Source:** `law-firm-audit-findings-2026.html`  
**Post date:** May 11, 2026  
**Link:** https://netwebmedia.com/blog/law-firm-audit-findings-2026.html

**Tweet 1 [HOOK]**
[THREAD] We audited 50 law firm websites for AEO readiness. The results: most firms are losing visibility in ChatGPT not because their content is bad, but because they haven't told AI engines how to cite them. Here's what we found.

**Tweet 2 [STAT 1]**
The schema crisis: 78% of audited firms lack FAQPage markup. That's 39 firms invisible for common legal questions. FAQPage is the single highest-ROI markup for law firms. ChatGPT looking for "What should I do if I'm fired?" will cite the firm with 12+ Q&A items—not the 2000-word blog post.

**Tweet 3 [STAT 2]**
Attorney schema: 62% of firms had zero Attorney markup. No bar membership declared. No practice areas listed. No jurisdiction signals. Result: 3.8x fewer citations vs a competitor with full schema on the same content.

**Tweet 4 [STAT 3]**
91% of firms published only generic practice area pages. "Criminal Defense" or "Divorce Law"—boilerplate, no differentiation. The 9% that stood out: jurisdiction-specific guides + step-by-step process pages + cost explainers. AEO simulation tests showed 2.1x higher rankings.

**Tweet 5 [STAT 4]**
Review velocity: 68% of firms had under 100 reviews. Average recency: 7+ months. Firms with 200+ fresh reviews convert legal AI queries at 3.2x the rate. The gap grows every month.

**Tweet 6 [CTA]**
The fixes aren't hard. FAQPage on 5 pages: 2 hours. Missing schema fields: 1 hour. One jurisdiction guide per quarter: 4-6 hours/month. Firms moving first on these in 2026 will own AI citation by 2027. Full audit data: https://netwebmedia.com/blog/law-firm-audit-findings-2026.html

### Thread 3: Local SEO vs AEO
**Source:** `law-firm-local-seo-vs-aeo.html`  
**Post date:** May 18, 2026  
**Link:** https://netwebmedia.com/blog/law-firm-local-seo-vs-aeo.html

**Tweet 1 [HOOK]**
[THREAD] "Should we focus on Local SEO or AEO?" We get this weekly. Answer: both. But the ranking is clear. Local SEO is the 80% channel right now. AEO is the 18% channel growing 40% YoY. Here's where to start.

**Tweet 2 [STAT 1]**
Local SEO (Google Maps + organic local pack) still drives 80% of law firm AI-era visibility. Someone searching "employment lawyer near me" is 2-3 days from calling. That's 18-35% conversion, high-intent. Google Local Pack gets 20-40% of clicks on local legal queries.

**Tweet 3 [STAT 2]**
AEO is the fast-growing second channel. ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini now handle 18% of informational search traffic. Less tied to geography initially—early movers with deep authority content get outsized advantage. It's 40% YoY growth.

**Tweet 4 [INSIGHT 1]**
Content overlap exists. A jurisdiction-specific guide ("Divorce in California: 7-step timeline") works for both. Local SEO needs location pages per jurisdiction. AEO rewards depth—1 authority page + FAQPage schema beats 5 thin location pages.

**Tweet 5 [INSIGHT 2]**
Where they differ: Local SEO cares about GMB, reviews, citation consistency. AEO cares about schema, attorney credentials, E-E-A-T. A perfect GMB without FAQPage won't get AI citations. Deep AEO content without reviews won't dominate the map.

**Tweet 6 [CTA]**
Decision tree: Single location + walk-in traffic? Prioritize Local SEO first. Multi-state + referrals? Lead with AEO. 8+ attorneys? Do both in parallel. Strategy + breakdown: https://netwebmedia.com/blog/law-firm-local-seo-vs-aeo.html #AEO #LocalSEO #Legal

## 4. Repurposing Template (Reusable System)

Use this template for any future NWM blog post → social conversion.

### Twitter Thread Template

**Slot 1: [HOOK]**  
Contrarian stat or question. Lead with the answer. Max 280 chars.

**Slot 2: [STAT 1]**  
Primary data point or claim. Make it specific (% or number).

**Slot 3: [INSIGHT 1]**  
How/why it matters. One idea per tweet.

**Slot 4: [INSIGHT 2]**  
Second implication or related idea.

**Slot 5: [INSIGHT 3]**  
Practical next step or third angle.

**Slot 6: [CTA]**  
Link + 1-2 hashtags. Nothing more.

**Rules:**
- First tweet is "Reply with [THREAD]" marker (optional, tests show similar performance)
- Each tweet ≤280 chars
- No hashtag spam (max 2 on CTA tweet only)
- Cite real numbers from the post (not estimates)
- Active voice only
- If tweet feels fluffy, cut it

---

### Instagram Carousel Template

**Slide 1 (Hook):** 8-word headline + 15-word body + visual direction  
**Slides 2–6:** Key insights (one per slide), 6-word headline + 20-word body  
**Slide 7 (CTA):** URL only + visual call-to-action  

**Visuals:**
- Slide 1: Navy dark navy bg (#010F3B), white text, one orange accent element
- Slides 2–6: Data slides use orange (#FF671F) for stats, navy for backgrounds; insight slides use card layout on navy with orange borders
- Slide 7: Navy button visual, orange text, clear URL

**Copy rules:**
- Headline is scannable (headline case)
- Body is 1–2 sentences max
- Every slide should stand alone (muted on scroll)
- Never more than 7 slides
- CTA is always the last slide

---

### WhatsApp Broadcast Template

**Length:** 130–160 words  
**Tone:** Warm, direct, one-to-one (avoid "we want to tell you")  
**Structure:**
1. Hook (1–2 sentences): What they should know
2. Data (3–4 bullets or short sentences): The evidence
3. So what (1 sentence): Why it matters
4. What to do (1 sentence): Next step
5. Link: Always include the blog URL
6. Soft CTA: "Let us know what comes up" or "Questions?"

**Copy rules:**
- Write like messaging a friend, not an email blast
- Contractions encouraged (it's, we're, don't)
- No emojis (brand uses text-based directness)
- One clear link, always at the end
- Skip the logo/signature—this is peer-to-peer
- Test on mobile first (WhatsApp text wrapping)

## 6. Publication & Distribution Notes

### Twitter/X
- Publish thread first (9 AM ET, Tuesday)
- Retweet from @netwebmedia account 24hrs later (for algorithmic boost)
- Pin thread for 48 hours post-launch
- Reply to thread with "bookmark this" engagement bait (light touch—avoid desperation)
- Quote-tweet competitor comparisons (e.g., quote a HubSpot post with "We built this differently")

### Instagram
- Publish carousel as feed post (Wed 10 AM ET)
- 48-hour story repurpose (same data, vertical format)
- Save carousel for repost every 6 weeks (audience overlap is minimal)
- Include link in bio for first 24 hours, then rotate
- Caption copy is same as slide 1 headline + body + CTA link

### WhatsApp
- Send only to warm segment (past attendees + audit requesters + free trial users)
- Segment by niche if possible (law firm list = this cluster)
- Send Tuesday 10 AM PT (highest open rates for this audience)
- Do NOT send more than 1 message per week to the same list
- Monitor unsubscribe rate; if >3%, pause and review

### Cross-posting Rules
- Never post all 3 formats on the same day to same channel (looks spammy)
- Repackage, don't republish (different headline, different angle, different data lead)
- If sharing a thread on LinkedIn (if policy changes), edit it into a single-post carousel (LinkedIn hates thread replies)

## 8. Voice Guardrails for This Cluster

- **Sharp, not snarky.** "78% of firms are missing this" is fine. "LOL firms be sleeping on schema" is not.
- **Specific data always beats clever wordplay.** A number or stat wins every time.
- **No hedging.** "Most law firms could benefit from..." → "Law firms are losing visibility because..."
- **Active voice mandatory.** "Schema is missing" → "You're missing schema."
- **Citations lead insights.** Start with the stat, then explain why it matters. Reverse order = preaching.
