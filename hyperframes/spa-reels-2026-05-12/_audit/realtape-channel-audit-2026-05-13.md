## Recommendations — ranked by impact

### TIER 1 — ship in the next 7 days (highest ROI)

1. **Cross-publish the 40 Spa reels as YouTube Shorts.** Format: vertical 1080×1920, ≤60s, hook in first 2s, branded `@REALTAPE` overlay. YouTube Shorts is currently rewarding sim-racing content at far higher CTR than the main feed. This is the single highest-leverage move.
2. **Rename "RealTape Reel #1" → "iRacing #1 | IMSA at Spa | The Start"** etc. Generic "Reel #1" titles win zero search impressions. Title format that works: `<game> | <car/series> | <track> | <hook>`.
3. **Make 1 edited "Race Highlights" video per livestream** (5–8 min). Title: `iRacing IMSA Multiclass — Spa-Francorchamps | Best Battles, Saves & Overtakes`. Upload within 48h of the livestream. Cost: 2–3 hours editing. Payoff: this is the format your 48%-retention reel proves works.

### TIER 2 — fix the channel-level layers (next 30 days)

4. **Channel banner + about page rewrite.** Make the first line on the about page read: *"iRacing simulator races. Real wheel-to-wheel battles, raw onboard, no gimmicks. New uploads weekly."* Right now there's no description committing to a niche.
5. **Hide or unlist pre-2025 misc content.** Tapati Rapa Nui, Eclipse 2019, Oasis concert, NASCAR — they're great memories, not channel-aligned. Set to Unlisted (keep them, but pull them out of the public grid) so the algorithm reads you as a sim-racing channel.
6. **Define a posting cadence.** Two committed slots: one livestream per week (existing rhythm) + 2 Shorts per week (new). Calendar it.
7. **Add chapters to every livestream.** YouTube weights chapter-rich videos higher. Each lap, pit, key overtake = a chapter. The Spa Porsche stream from today should have chapters retrofitted.
8. **End-screen + pinned-comment workflow.** Every livestream pins a comment with timestamps to the 3 best moments and links to the corresponding Shorts. Every Short ends with a 1s end-card pointing to the full race.

### TIER 3 — narrative + community (next 90 days)

9. **Pick a recurring series.** Something the algorithm can latch onto. Three I'd test:
   - *"Road to 2000 iRating"* — you already named one livestream this; turn it into the channel's spine. Each stream is a new chapter. Each reel is a moment from that journey.
   - *"Real Track vs Sim Track"* — when you race Spa in iRacing, also drive Spa in your VW one weekend (you already have the Nürburgring footage). High-novelty crossover content.
   - *"Driver of the Day"* — call out one driver per stream that earned your respect. Builds community + invites driver tags.
10. **Comments → content loop.** A current top comment on the Spa stream says *"Please lower your force feedback, it's clipping like 90% of the time."* That's a free Short idea: "I had my force feedback wrong — here's the fix." 30-second talking head + setup screen. Comment authors usually become subs when they see their feedback addressed.
11. **Membership / Patreon hook is premature.** Build to 5K subs first. Don't gate content yet.
