## ⚠️ Data quality flag — USA master

82% of the USA master (167,788 / 204,671 rows) uses just five hardcoded email local-parts: `info`, `contact`, `hello`, `sales`, `admin`. Real scraped data never distributes this evenly across only 5 prefixes. This pattern strongly suggests `api-php/data/usa_best_200.csv` and `usa_5x.csv` were generated by attaching plausible-looking person names to template emails — not scraped from real business listings.

**Implications:**
- Names attached to these rows are likely fabricated. Personalized cold email using `{first_name}` tokens will read as a mismatch ("Hi Leah" sent to `info@aceauto.com` where there is no Leah).
- Deliverability is OK (the `info@` mailboxes exist), but you'll burn sender reputation if you over-volume them.
- Treat as a **company-level** prospect list (B2B accounts) rather than a contact-level personal outreach list. Pair with a real owner/contact lookup (Hunter, Apollo, manual GMB) before personalized outreach.

The ~36K rows with non-templated local-parts (office, team, support, reservations, frontdesk, catering, etc.) are higher-quality and likely real.

## Rules applied (strict non-human only)

**Dropped:**
- Empty email, `Not found`, `N/A`, malformed addresses
- Non-human locals: noreply, no-reply, do-not-reply, postmaster, webmaster,   mailer-daemon, abuse, bounce, unsubscribe, root, daemon, nobody
- Junk domains: example.com, test.com, localhost, yourdomain.com
- Rows missing **both** a name/company **and** a website
- Duplicate emails (first occurrence wins, case-insensitive)

**Kept (Carlos rule, 2026-05-14):**
- Role-based SMB inboxes: `info@`, `contacto@`, `contact@`, `ventas@`,   `sales@`, `reservas@`, `hello@`, `admin@`, `support@` — these are the   legitimate way to reach the 14-niche SMB target base.
