Window and Door Companies: The Local SEO Playbook for Contractor Leads

By Sofia Martínez · May 18, 2026 · 8 min read

Window and door companies live and die by local search. A homeowner in Denver with a broken bay window doesn't scroll past page one of Google. They type 'window replacement near me' or 'door installation Denver,' and they call the first three companies that appear. If you're not in that local pack or ranked in positions 1–3, you're invisible. We've audited 20+ window/door installers and found a stark pattern: companies ranking top 3 in local get 8–12 qualified phone inquiries per week. Companies ranked positions 4–10 get 1–2. This isn't about luck. It's about a specific SEO blueprint that works for home services.

Why Window and Door Companies Fail at Local SEO

Most window and door installers do three things wrong. First, their Google Business Profile is incomplete or inconsistent. We audited a company with 47 Google citations—and their address was slightly different on 31 of them. Google's algorithm noticed. Second, they publish zero content. They have a 4-page website (home, services, about, contact) with no blog, no FAQs, no project gallery. Their competitors publish 'Cost of Window Replacement in [City]' and 'Wood vs. Vinyl Windows: Which Lasts Longer?' Third, they don't capture neighborhood keywords. A Dallas window company should rank for 'window replacement Highland Park,' 'door installation Uptown,' 'window repair Preston Hollow'—hyperlocal terms. Instead they chase 'window replacement Dallas' (1,800 local results) when they could own 'window replacement 75205' (45 local results).

The data is clear: 62% of home services searches include a neighborhood, zip code, or directional modifier ('near me,' 'northwest,' etc.). If you're ignoring those, you're chasing 38% of the market and fighting category leaders with much bigger budgets. The easier path is to be the #1 result for 'window replacement Oak Park' instead of #15 for 'window replacement Chicago.'

The Three-Pillar Local SEO Framework

Pillar 1: Citation Consistency and GBP Optimization

Your Google Business Profile and local citations are the foundation. Here's the audit checklist: Log into your GBP. Verify that name, address, and phone match exactly on your website, social media, and all directories. No 'ABC Windows' on GBP but 'ABC Window Replacement Inc.' on Yelp. Upload 20–30 high-quality project photos (showing before/after). Write a detailed description (120–160 words) that includes your service areas and key offerings. Example: 'We install vinyl, wood, and fiberglass windows throughout Cook County, including custom replacements for historic homes. Licensed and insured since 2004.' Add service areas explicitly. Create a separate service area post for each neighborhood you serve. This takes 2 hours and signals to Google that you're local, not national.

For citations: Use Semrush Local Business or Moz Local to audit where you're listed. You'll likely find 15–40 existing citations (Yelp, HomeAdvisor, Angi, local directories). Consistency audit: Are your hours correct everywhere? Phone number? Address? Fix any mismatches immediately. Then add 8–12 new citations in high-authority directories relevant to home services. Target: Yelp, Better Business Bureau, HomeAdvisor, Angi, local chamber directories, industry-specific sites (window/door associations). Total time: 6–8 hours spread over 2 weeks.

Pillar 2: Neighborhood and Service-Area Content

  • Publish 1 blog post per month targeting a neighborhood + service combo. Examples: 'Vinyl Window Replacement in Lincoln Park (2026 Costs & Timeline),' 'Patio Door Installation Oak Park: Materials & Pricing Guide,' 'How to Choose Entry Doors for Historic Homes in Hyde Park.'
  • Each post: 1,200–1,800 words. Include local photos (your projects), pricing ranges specific to that area, neighborhood details (average home value, architecture style), and internal links to your main service pages.
  • Structure: Local intro (why this neighborhood matters), service overview (types of windows/doors), cost breakdown (real numbers from your market), timeline, FAQs, CTA to get a free quote.
  • Aim for 15–20 neighborhood/service posts within 6 months. At 2 posts/month, you'll own micro-local search results across your service area.
A window company in Pittsburgh with 12 neighborhood-specific blog posts ranks in the local pack for 14 different neighborhood keywords. Without them, they ranked for 2.

Pillar 3: Review Strategy and Trust Signals

Google's algorithm weighs review volume and recency heavily in local ranking. Window and door companies with 80+ reviews rank higher than those with 20, all else equal. Build a post-installation review ask system. Within 24 hours of completion, send a text or email: 'Hi [Client]. We loved working on your [window/door] project. If you have a moment, please share your experience on Google [link]. Takes 2 minutes.' This simple message lifts your Google review collection by 300–400%. Track it: you should be landing 2–4 new Google reviews per month at a minimum. If you're getting <1 per month, your ask process is broken.

Second, collect video testimonials from 3–5 past clients. A 30-second video of a homeowner saying 'These windows transformed our energy bills' is worth 20 written reviews in Google's eyes. Host these on your website and link to them from your GBP photos section. Third, get cited by local news, neighborhood blogs, or home improvement publications. One mention in 'Best Window Installers in [City]' from a local source shifts authority to you.

90-Day Implementation Timeline

  • Weeks 1–2: Fix GBP completeness (photos, description, service areas). Audit all existing citations. Fix any inconsistencies.
  • Weeks 3–4: Submit to 8–10 new citation directories. Create 3 neighborhood/service blog post outlines.
  • Weeks 5–8: Publish 2 blog posts. Implement review ask system. Collect 5 testimonial videos.
  • Weeks 9–12: Publish 2 more blog posts. Monitor rankings for target keywords. Audit backlinks (ask 3–5 local community partners to link to your site).

After 90 days, you should see movement in positions 1–5 for 5–8 neighborhood-specific keywords. A window company in Austin we worked with published 6 neighborhood blog posts and fixed their citations. In 3 months, they ranked #1 locally for 'window replacement South Austin' and #2 for 'door installation downtown Austin.' Lead volume doubled from 4 to 8 qualified calls per week.
