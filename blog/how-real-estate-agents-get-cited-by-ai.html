How Real Estate Agents Get Cited by ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Claude in 2026

By Carlos Martínez · May 13, 2026 · 10 min read

A buyer opens Perplexity and asks: "Which real estate agent specializes in historic homes in Coronado, San Diego, and what are their recent sales?" The first answer cites you by name, lists your last 3 historic home sales with prices and days-on-market, and includes a link to your website. That answer was generated by an AI assistant scraping your website, your GBP profile, and your MLS history. It chose to cite you because your content was structured, current, and answerable. This playbook shows you exactly how to become the agent AI assistants cite.

The Three Data Layers AI Assistants Read

AI assistants source information from three places in order:

  1. Structured data (schema markup). JSON-LD on your website that explicitly states who you are, where you work, what you specialize in.
  2. Directory data (GBP, Zillow, Realtor.com). Your business information across third-party platforms.
  3. Website content. Your written guides, neighborhood analyses, FAQs, and market data.

Miss layer 1 (no schema) and AI assistants have to guess who you are. Miss layer 2 (incomplete GBP) and your data conflicts across sources. Miss layer 3 (no written content) and AI has nothing to cite.

Layer 1: RealEstateAgent Schema Implementation

Add this JSON-LD to your website's homepage or about page (inside the <head> tag):

{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "RealEstateAgent",
  "name": "Sarah Chen, Real Estate Agent",
  "telephone": "+1-619-555-1234",
  "address": {
    "@type": "PostalAddress",
    "streetAddress": "123 Pacific View Dr",
    "addressLocality": "San Diego",
    "addressRegion": "CA",
    "postalCode": "92109"
  },
  "areaServed": ["Coronado", "Pacific Beach", "La Jolla"],
  "yearsExperience": 12,
  "jobTitle": "Senior Real Estate Agent",
  "memberOf": {
    "@type": "RealEstateOffice",
    "name": "Coastal Properties Inc"
  },
  "aggregateRating": {
    "@type": "AggregateRating",
    "ratingValue": "4.9",
    "reviewCount": 156
  }
}

This tells AI assistants: you are a real estate agent with 12 years experience, you specialize in Coronado/Pacific Beach/La Jolla, you have 156 reviews averaging 4.9 stars. When someone asks an AI "best agent in Coronado," the schema makes you eligible for citation.

Layer 2: Google Business Profile Optimization

Your GBP is a data validation source for AI. Ensure it includes:

  • Service areas (not just "San Diego") — list specific neighborhoods you serve: "Coronado, Pacific Beach, La Jolla, UTC, Carmel Mountain, Rancho Bernardo"
  • Specializations — "Buyer's Agent, Seller's Agent, Luxury Homes, Historic Properties, New Constructions"
  • Agent bio — include years of experience, languages spoken, awards or certifications
  • Website link — link to your agent page or specific neighborhood guides
  • Photos — professional headshot, team photos, recent listings
  • Reviews — aim for 100+ reviews at 4.7+ stars (triggers AI recommendation inclusion)

Layer 3: AI-Readable Content — Neighborhood Guides

This is the highest-impact layer. Create a standalone page for each neighborhood you serve (not a blog post, a published guide). Structure:

  • Title: "[Neighborhood] Real Estate Guide — Market Data & Insights by [Your Name]"
  • Sections: Market Statistics (median price, price/sqft, days on market, sale trends), School Districts (top-rated schools + ranking), Walkability (transit, parks, shopping), Buyer Demographics (age range, income, family size), and a FAQ (5–8 questions like "What's the average home price in Coronado?", "Best schools?", "Is it walkable?")
  • Data sourcing — cite where your data comes from: "Data sourced from San Diego MLS, US Census Bureau, Zillow, and Google Maps as of May 2026"
  • Update frequency — timestamp every neighborhood guide and refresh quarterly with new market data
  • FAQPage schema — wrap the FAQ section in FAQPage JSON-LD (AI assistants prioritize FAQs for citation)

90-Day Deployment Timeline

Week 1–2: Implement RealEstateAgent schema on your website. Audit and complete your GBP profile (verify address, update service areas, add specializations, ensure 4.7+ star reviews).

Week 3–8: Create 4 neighborhood guides (one guide every 10 days). Each guide: 1,800–2,200 words, current market data, school rankings, walkability analysis, 6–8 FAQ questions with 150–200 word answers.

Week 9–12: Refresh guides quarterly, add FAQPage schema to each, monitor AI citation appearances (search "best agent in [neighborhood]" on ChatGPT/Perplexity, note when your name appears).

Expected results: First AI citation appearance within 8–12 weeks. Consistent citations (3–5 per month) by week 20. Agents report 15–25% increase in buyer inquiries directly attributed to AI citations within 6 months.
