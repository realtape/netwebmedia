# Project Proposal: Blog Publishing and Prompt Management

Date: March 30, 2026

Prepared for: Tammy Fuscher, Shan, Chad

Prepared by: Carlos Martinez

## Executive summary

This proposal recommends two tightly connected projects that can be delivered in a contractor format with clear ownership, deadlines, and measurable outputs:

1. A blog production and publishing system that turns approved topics into review-ready and publish-ready articles on a dependable schedule.
2. A prompt management system that centralizes the instructions, quality standards, and review workflow used by AI-assisted content creation.

Together, these projects address the current bottleneck identified in the meeting: content can be drafted, but it does not consistently move from idea to approved and published asset. The recommendation is to prioritize the blog production project first and run prompt management alongside it because the second project improves speed, consistency, and handoff quality for the first.

## Current bottlenecks

- Content creation is moving toward AI-assisted workflows, but there is not yet a clear, centralized prompt system for repeatable output quality.
- Blog production is at risk of delay if content, review, publishing, and ownership are not defined end to end.
- Publishing velocity has been inconsistent in the past, which reduces the value of drafts that never make it to the website.
- The team is moving to Notion for task management, so content work needs clear ownership, due dates, and approval stages.
- HubSpot is the preferred direction for blog/content workflows, but the website and CTA strategy still need coordination.

## Proposal 1: Blog Production and Publishing System

### Objective

Create a repeatable workflow that produces useful, free blog content tied to business goals, with each article moving from brief to draft to review to publication on a defined timeline.

### Business value

- Increases the amount of free educational content available to support brand trust, SEO, and lead generation.
- Creates a reliable pipeline instead of one-off drafts.
- Gives the team a clear view of what is being written, reviewed, and published.
- Supports AI-assisted production without sacrificing human review and business alignment.

### Scope

- Build a blog production workflow for topic selection, brief creation, drafting, review, revision, and publishing.
- Propose an initial editorial slate of 6 blog posts aligned to target services and likely search intent.
- Write or coordinate AI-assisted first drafts for the first 3 priority posts.
- Create article briefs that define target audience, keyword/theme, CTA, internal links, and approval owner.
- Define what content links to, including service pages, contact CTAs, or downloadable resources where applicable.
- Package all work in Notion-ready task stages so ownership and due dates are visible.
- Coordinate publishing handoff requirements with Daniel or the appropriate web owner.

### Deliverables

- 6-post editorial plan with priorities and business intent
- 3 review-ready blog drafts
- 3 article briefs for the next batch
- Publishing checklist for each post
- Notion task structure for content status tracking

### Estimated effort

- Editorial planning and topic selection: 4 hours
- Brief creation for 6 posts: 6 hours
- Drafting and revision for first 3 posts: 12 to 15 hours
- Publishing coordination and QA handoff: 3 hours
- Total: 25 to 28 hours

### Timeline

- March 31, 2026: finalize topics, goals, CTA mapping, and owners
- April 1 to April 3, 2026: create briefs and draft the first 2 posts
- April 4 to April 5, 2026: draft the third post and revise based on review feedback
- April 6 to April 7, 2026: prepare publishing packages and confirm handoff with the web owner

### Requirements and dependencies

- Final decision on publishing path: HubSpot blog, WordPress, or website-integrated workflow
- Access to existing brand voice guidance, service priorities, and any SEO targets
- Named reviewer/approver for content signoff
- Coordination with Daniel or the person responsible for publishing
- Notion board or equivalent task tracking in place

### Success metrics

- 6 approved blog topics prioritized
- 3 review-ready posts completed within the agreed schedule
- 100% of drafted posts mapped to a CTA and business goal
- Clear owner assigned for every stage from draft to publish

## Recommended initial blog topics

## Proposal 2: Prompt Management and AI Content Operations

### Objective

Create a centralized prompt management system so AI-generated content follows the same strategic instructions, brand rules, quality checks, and approval workflow every time.

### Business value

- Reduces inconsistency across AI-generated content.
- Prevents loss of knowledge by documenting prompt logic and role-based instructions.
- Speeds up drafting while making review easier for the marketing team.
- Creates a reusable content operations asset that supports future agents and workflows.

### Scope

- Audit the current AI content workflow and identify where prompts, instructions, and review standards are currently scattered.
- Create a prompt library for core content tasks such as topic generation, article briefing, first-draft writing, revision, repurposing, and QA review.
- Define a standard prompt structure including objective, audience, format, source inputs, brand rules, prohibited claims, CTA requirements, and review checklist.
- Create an approval-ready operating document that explains when to use each prompt and what a human must still review before publishing.
- Organize this system for use in Notion or another shared documentation space.

### Deliverables

- Central prompt library for blog and related content tasks
- Prompt template framework for future AI agents
- Quality control checklist for human review
- Recommended workflow for prompt updates, versioning, and ownership
- Notion-ready documentation structure

### Estimated effort

- Workflow audit and prompt inventory: 4 hours
- Prompt library design and drafting: 8 hours
- QA checklist and governance structure: 4 hours
- Documentation and team handoff: 2 hours
- Total: 18 hours

### Timeline

- March 31 to April 1, 2026: audit current materials and define content task categories
- April 2 to April 3, 2026: draft the core prompt library and review checklist
- April 4, 2026: organize documentation and finalize the handoff package

### Requirements and dependencies

- Access to current prompt examples, brand guidance, and sample outputs
- Agreement on who owns approval for new prompt versions
- Alignment on where prompt documentation will live, preferably in Notion

### Success metrics

- One shared prompt library for priority content tasks
- One approved review checklist used before publication
- Reduced revision cycles caused by inconsistent AI outputs
- Faster turnaround from topic to first usable draft

## Recommended delivery approach

The strongest contractor proposal is to position these projects as one practical content operations package with two phases:

### Phase 1: Blog production launch

### Phase 2: Prompt management foundation

Document the prompt system and quality controls that make future output faster, more consistent, and easier to scale.

This sequencing shows immediate business value while also building the infrastructure needed for the team's AI-led content strategy.
