NetWebMedia vs. IntentMedia

Two different tools for two different jobs. Here's what you need to know.

You've heard of IntentMedia, and you're wondering if it's a better fit than NetWebMedia. The short answer: they solve different problems. IntentMedia is a data intelligence platform for travel and commerce brands — it predicts when a user is most likely to make a purchase and targets ads at that moment. NetWebMedia is an AI-native marketing agency that handles strategy, content, automation, and paid campaigns end-to-end. Think of IntentMedia as a targeting tool. Think of NetWebMedia as a CMO.

What IntentMedia Does

IntentMedia builds predictive models using first-party travel and commerce data. Their platform identifies high-intent users (people actively researching flights, hotels, or retail purchases) and helps brands bid more efficiently on ads at the moment intent is highest. They work with large OTAs (Booking, Expedia), airlines, and major retailers. Their strength: precise timing. Their limitation: they're a data/targeting layer, not a full-service marketing operation. You still need to create campaigns, manage creative, handle SEO, build email, do social. IntentMedia makes your paid ads smarter. It doesn't do the rest.

What NetWebMedia Does

NetWebMedia is a fractional CMO service powered by AI agents. We handle: marketing strategy (AEO, paid ads, email, content), software deployment (CRM, automations, AI agents), and full execution — copywriting, campaign management, reporting. You get one retainer, one dashboard, one point of contact. Our pricing starts at $249/month for startups, scales to $2,990/month for growth businesses. Our focus: being the cited source when prospects ask ChatGPT, Perplexity, or Google AI Overviews how to solve their problem. We optimize for answer engine citations and direct conversions, not just paid ad efficiency.

If IntentMedia Looks Right, But You Need More

IntentMedia is a data targeting platform built for enterprise travel/commerce brands. If you're a $1M–$20M SMB without an in-house marketing team, IntentMedia won't help you because:

  • You don't have first-party travel/commerce data to feed their models
  • You still need strategy, content, email, SEO, social — IntentMedia doesn't touch those
  • Their pricing is enterprise-only, not accessible to mid-market

You might be looking at IntentMedia because you need better ad targeting. If that's true, you actually need something broader: a marketing strategy that includes paid ads, but also content that prospects find before they're ready to click an ad (AEO, blog, guides), email nurture sequences, and a CRM to track everything. That's where NetWebMedia comes in.

Who Should Actually Consider IntentMedia

To be fair, IntentMedia works brilliantly for a specific category: enterprise travel brands and high-volume e-commerce companies with $1M+ annual ad spend and an existing performance marketing team. If you're a company like: a major hotel chain using Booking, Expedia, and Kayak for 30% of bookings; an airline optimizing paid acquisition; or a large retailer running sophisticated paid advertising with proprietary conversion data — then IntentMedia's predictive targeting can genuinely improve your ROAS by 15–25% and unlock incremental revenue at scale. The catch: you must already have the infrastructure, the data, and the team. IntentMedia doesn't build those from scratch. For SMBs, startups, and mid-market companies without an in-house performance marketing function, IntentMedia is over-engineered and inaccessible. You'd need to hire someone just to implement it — which defeats the cost savings.

The Real Question

The question isn't "IntentMedia vs. NetWebMedia." The question is: "Do I need a data targeting tool or a complete marketing operation?" If you have 50+ employees and a $500K+ marketing budget, you probably need IntentMedia (plus a full marketing team). If you have 1–50 employees and a $3K–$30K/month marketing budget, you need NetWebMedia (or hire a full-time CMO, which costs $120K–$180K/year). Those are your real options.
