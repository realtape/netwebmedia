01Brand North Star

Purpose

Democratize Fortune-500 marketing leadership for small and mid-sized businesses by compressing a 10-person department into one AI-native retainer.

Vision (3-year)

Be the default answer when ChatGPT or Perplexity is asked “how does a small business run marketing with AI?”

Mission

Help SMB founders stop worrying about marketing — by shipping strategy, software, and execution under one roof, at one transparent price.

Values (operating, not poster)

  • Revenue or silence. Every marketing act must tie to pipeline or trust-building. No activity for activity’s sake.
  • Ship the brand, not the busywork. Bias to publish. Bias to deploy. Decks don’t move the needle.
  • Own the tools, not the margin loss. We build our own stack because renting agency margin to SaaS is a dead end.
  • Bilingual by default. English + Spanish is native, not an afterthought.
  • Honesty is speed. We tell clients what’s not working — in week 2, not quarter 2.

Brand Promise

“One retainer. AI + human. You stop worrying about marketing.”

03Voice & Tone

Five voice principles

1. Sentences earn their space

Do

“Your site doesn’t show up in ChatGPT. Here’s the fix.”

Don’t

“In today’s rapidly evolving digital landscape, it’s more important than ever to ensure that your brand has visibility across emerging AI-powered discovery channels.”

2. Specific over clever

Do

“847 ChatGPT citations in 90 days.”

Don’t

“Unleash the power of AI citations.”

3. Price tags without squirming

Do

“$249/month. No setup fee.”

Don’t

“Contact us for pricing tailored to your needs.”

4. First-person plural, rarely first-person singular

Do

“We shipped 847 citations in 90 days.”

Don’t

“I founded NetWebMedia to bring enterprise marketing to SMBs.”

Exception: Carlos bylines. Personal essays use I.

5. Humor is OK if it’s true

Do

“We asked ChatGPT about your niche. It has never heard of you.”

Don’t

“LOL marketers be like 😂”

Tone shifts by context

ContextToneExample opener
Sales pageConfident, specific, numeric“Get cited by ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google — starting at $249/mo.”
Blog articleAnalytical, data-first, occasionally sharp“We pulled 1,000 ChatGPT citations. Here’s what actually moves rankings.”
Customer emailDirect, warm, brief“Your Q1 results are in. Quick read, one decision needed.”
Error messagePlain, ownership, no jargon“That didn’t work. We logged it. Here’s what to try next.”
Social postObservational, one-line hooks“HubSpot is $3,600/mo. Then you hire an agency. Make it make sense.”

07Logo Usage

NetWebMedia.

Canonical logo files: assets/images/netwebmedia-logo-lockup.png (horizontal) and assets/images/nwm-logo.svg (mark).

Clear space

Minimum clear space around the logo = the height of the lowercase “n” on all four sides. Never let anything cross that boundary.

Minimum sizes

  • Digital: 120px wide (lockup), 32px wide (mark)
  • Print: 1 inch wide (lockup), 0.5 inch (mark)

Approved color versions

  • Full color — Navy + Orange — on white or light cream backgrounds
  • Reversed — White + Orange — on NWM Navy Dark or any dark photograph
  • Monochrome — All Navy or All White — for single-color print or favicons

Six misuse “don’ts”

  1. Don’t stretch or distort proportions.
  2. Don’t recolor outside the approved palette.
  3. Don’t place on busy photography without a backing panel.
  4. Don’t rotate more than 0°.
  5. Don’t add drop shadows, outer glows, or bevels.
  6. Don’t re-typeset the wordmark in a different font.

08Iconography

Library: Lucide. 1,400+ icons, stroke-only, consistent weight, active maintenance.

Why Lucide over Phosphor or Heroicons: Phosphor’s thin/bold/duotone variants create inconsistency when multiple contributors touch it. Heroicons is too small a library. Lucide is boring in the best way.

Usage rules

  • Stroke width: 1.5 (default), 2 for emphasis only
  • Standard size: 20px UI, 24px feature, 16px inline
  • Color: match text color or use --nwm-orange for accent
  • Never mix icon libraries in the same screen

09Photography & Imagery

Direction

High-contrast, dark-background, rim-lit subjects. This is the only photography style that integrates cleanly against a #010F3B navy backdrop without heavy retouching.

What works

  • Low-key portraits with a single strong edge light (product-hero feel)
  • Desk + device compositions shot in shadow, not flat-lay overhead daylight
  • Abstract motion blur (car livery, track photography) — references Gulf heritage without being on-the-nose

What doesn’t

  • Bright lifestyle stock (laptop + coffee, diverse-team-around-whiteboard)
  • Handshake metaphors, arrow-hitting-bullseye metaphors, hooded-hacker metaphors
  • Any image with its own dominant color palette that fights the brand palette

AI-generated imagery (Midjourney, fal, Ideogram)

Prompt seeds that fit:

  • …, cinematic rim-light, single key, navy backdrop, gulf-orange accent, moody, shallow depth of field, 35mm
  • …, editorial product shot, dark studio, orange bounce light, sharp focus, Poppins-era minimalism

Never ship an AI image that shows fake hands, fake text on screens, or fake logos. Zero tolerance.

12Motion

Easing

  • Default: cubic-bezier(0.4, 0, 0.2, 1) (Material standard)
  • Entrance: cubic-bezier(0, 0, 0.2, 1) (decelerate)
  • Exit: cubic-bezier(0.4, 0, 1, 1) (accelerate)

Duration tokens

  • 150ms — hover, focus, icon swap
  • 300ms — card lift, modal open
  • 500ms — section reveal, hero animations

What animates, what doesn’t

  • Animates: button hover, card lift, modal open, nav scroll shadow, typewriter hero
  • Never animates: body text fading in on scroll (exhausting), parallax images (distracting), icon spinning unless loading

The hero typewriter effect already on the site is canonical. Use it only for the hero subhead — not a page-wide pattern.

Reduced motion

Respect prefers-reduced-motion. When set, disable all non-essential animations. Keep focus states, keep essential loading states.

15Bilingual & Localization

NetWebMedia is bilingual by default — English for US, Spanish for LATAM. Localization is not translation.

Spanish tone shifts

  • LATAM Spanish, not Spain Spanish (celular, not móvil; computadora, not ordenador)
  • Usted is the default register for business prospects; tú only for startup founders under 35 or in casual social
  • Currency: USD everywhere in pricing copy; local currency only in paid ads geo-targeted to a specific country
  • Humor translates poorly — the “ChatGPT has never heard of you” line lands in English but needs softening in Spanish

What doesn’t change

The brand voice is the same person in both languages: direct, specific, performance-minded. Only the register shifts, not the personality.

16Accessibility

Baseline: WCAG 2.2 AA for all marketing pages, AAA for anything compliance-related.

Principles

  • Color never carries meaning alone. If something is “red”, it must also have an icon or label.
  • Keyboard-navigable everything. All CTAs, all modals, all nav — Tab, Shift+Tab, Enter, Esc all work.
  • Focus states must be visible. 2px orange outline, 2px offset — never remove focus rings.
  • Alt text is copy, not decoration. Describe what the image conveys, not “image of a laptop.”
  • Video must have captions. Every Carlos video. No exceptions.

17Brand Governance

Source of truth

This document. Lives in /plans/brand-book.html and the Markdown version at BRAND.md in the repo root.

Who approves new brand extensions

  • Carlos (CEO): any change to logo, palette, tagline, positioning
  • Creative Director agent: new ad creative, social templates, visual assets
  • Content Strategist agent: new voice applications, editorial templates
  • All agents: within the rules above, no approval needed

White-label / reseller applications

Partners who resell the software can whitelabel the software only. They do NOT whitelabel the NetWebMedia agency brand. Partner co-marketing uses the NWM wordmark + partner logo in a clear “X × NetWebMedia” lockup, never substituting one for the other.
