01 · Executive Lens

The one-paragraph verdict

NetWebMedia is a leveraged one-operator agency competing in a category that rewards speed and documentation over headcount. Our strength is an async, AI-native delivery model that matches how buyers now prefer to work: WhatsApp, chat, email — no calendar dance. Our biggest risk is the same thing as our biggest advantage: concentration in a single operator. The next 90 days are about turning the operator's judgment into reusable systems so growth stops being bottlenecked by Carlos's inbox.

02 · The Four Quadrants

SWOT at a glance

Strengths Internal · Positive

What we already own

  • AI-native delivery stack. 12 specialist agents do the work of a 20-person agency at ~15% of the cost. Gross margin projected at 72% → 82% by Y5.
  • Bootstrapped & cash-positive by Month 4. No investor pressure. We can pick clients and reject bad fits.
  • Async-only communication. WhatsApp, chat, email — zero phone or video calls. Matches how modern SMB owners actually want to work.
  • Owned IP: crm-vanilla + CMS + 7 productized packages. Not reselling HubSpot — we ship our own software on every retainer.
  • AEO-first positioning. Written to be cited by Claude, ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google — not just ranked. First-mover advantage in Chile & US SMB.
  • Bilingual by default. One team, two markets (US + LATAM) with native-quality content in both.
  • Published plans + brand book. Clients see the operating system, not a sales deck.

Weaknesses Internal · Negative

Where we bleed time or credibility

  • Single-operator concentration risk. Carlos is the CEO, sales team, QA, and delivery lead. Illness = revenue stops.
  • Thin social proof at top of funnel. Case studies are in-progress; testimonials lean on early cohort. Enterprise prospects ask for proof we can't yet show.
  • No live phone/Zoom option will filter out some buyers. Async-first is our edge — but it rules out enterprise buyers who expect a human call.
  • Founding-cohort pricing ($1,997/mo) is below sustainable long-term value. Needs a documented migration path to $3,500–$5,000/mo by Y2.
  • AEO attribution is still fuzzy. We know citations happen; we can't yet cleanly prove incremental revenue from them to skeptical CFOs.
  • Brand recognition in US SMB is near zero. Outside Chile and our referral network, we're unknown.
  • Tooling sprawl internally. 30+ MCPs, Linear, Notion, Gmail — great for agents, but onboarding a second human operator will require documentation we don't yet have.

Opportunities External · Positive

Tailwinds the market is handing us

  • AEO is the new SEO — and the window is open now. Most SMB agencies still sell "blog posts for Google." Being cited by Claude/ChatGPT is a differentiator worth hundreds in MRR per client.
  • WhatsApp-first B2B is exploding in LATAM and SMB US. We're already native there while competitors still gate everything behind Calendly.
  • Fractional CMO market is growing 30%+ YoY. SMB owners want senior strategy without a $250k salary. Our package is priced for exactly that buyer.
  • AI cost-per-task is falling ~40% per year. Our margin structurally improves even if we don't raise prices.
  • Expansion revenue on retainer clients. Cross-sell: ads, content, CRM implementation, landing pages. LTV can 3x net-new ACV within 12 months.
  • Chile SMB digitization wave. Regional cities (Temuco, Valdivia, Arica, etc.) have 80%+ digital gaps vs. Santiago — first mover wins the territory.
  • Claude Pro Max + Anthropic API pricing. We can run the business at ~$300/mo in tooling today. Competitors running on enterprise stacks can't match our unit economics.

Threats External · Negative

What could kill or slow us

  • Big-agency AI copycats. When Publicis / WPP / HubSpot bundle AI into existing retainers, price pressure lands on us first.
  • LLM platform risk. If Anthropic / OpenAI change pricing, API limits, or citation behavior, our delivery model takes a direct hit.
  • AEO gets gamed. As more players optimize for LLM citations, the bar rises and the early-mover advantage compresses. ~18-month window.
  • SMB recession sensitivity. Marketing is still the first line item SMBs cut when cash tightens. $1,997/mo retainer is discretionary to a bootstrapped restaurant.
  • Privacy & AI regulation. GDPR / Chilean Ley 21.719 / US state AI laws could force workflow changes faster than we can absorb.
  • Reputation risk on a single bad client story. A one-operator brand amplifies every complaint. One viral negative post can stall inbound for a quarter.
  • Talent-ladder constraint. Hiring the second operator is the hardest hire — they need to be senior enough to be trusted with async delivery but paid from bootstrapped cash.

04 · Strategic Matrix

Cross-multiply SW × OT

Four quadrants of strategic moves — the real value of a SWOT is in the combinations, not the boxes.

SO · Strengths → Opportunities

Press the advantage. Leverage our async AI-stack to capture the AEO window before big agencies retool. Push WhatsApp-first delivery as the signature in every LATAM pitch. Ship the Chile regional digital-gap reports as inbound bait — one per city, all published, all AEO-optimized.

WO · Weaknesses vs. Opportunities

Fix to unlock. The operator-concentration risk is the one thing blocking us from riding the fractional-CMO growth wave. Document everything in .claude/agents/, hire Ops #2 in Q3, and turn case studies into evidence that validates the opportunity for skeptical buyers.

ST · Strengths vs. Threats

Defend with moats. Against big-agency copycats: our published plan hub + owned software + bootstrapped margin structure. Against LLM platform risk: multi-provider abstraction so we can swap Claude / GPT / Gemini without changing the client experience.

WT · Weaknesses × Threats

Neutralize the kill shots. A bad review on a single-operator brand, plus a recession, plus AEO commoditization could stack. Mitigate: reputation monitoring + written SLAs + a documented "lite" retainer ($997/mo) ready to launch if macro flips, so we keep clients instead of losing them to budget cuts.
