3. How each video is made

Every video goes through these steps:

  1. Script — AI drafts. Humans edit. About 3 minutes per video.
  2. Footage — stock library, your existing footage, or AI-generated visuals (Runway, Sora).
  3. Voiceover — ElevenLabs plus your voice clone, or a selection of licensed voices.
  4. Edit — cuts, captions, music, transitions. Human editor, about 20 minutes per video.
  5. Review — internal check for brand, accuracy, and legal.
  6. Delivery — you get a ZIP file with all 30 videos plus a posting schedule.

4. Formats that work

Hook, teach, ask

12-second format. High watch-through. People save it.

Before and after

Screen recording showing your product solving a problem. Great for software.

Customer quote

30-second clip edited from a longer recording. Pure trust builder.

Founder's point of view

Selfie-style, you on camera sharing a strong opinion. Builds brand.

5. Posting them

We can post for you (included on Pro) or deliver files for your team. Posting schedule:

  • Weekdays at 9am and 6pm in your local time (tuned per account based on audience data).
  • Same video reposted to TikTok, Reels, Shorts, and LinkedIn with the caption rewritten per platform.
  • Top-performing videos boosted with $50 to $150 paid spend (optional).

6. Reporting

Weekly Monday report:

  • Views, reach, engagement per video.
  • Top 3 performers last week — we double down on those formats.
  • Bottom 3 — we learn from what flopped.
  • Follower growth, profile visits, outbound link clicks.

What success looks like

  • Within 30 days: 30 videos delivered, posting on schedule across all 4 platforms.
  • Within 60 days: at least one video pulling 10x your average reach.
  • Within 90 days: measurable follower growth, DMs from prospects, and videos re-used as paid ads.

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Demanding a revision on every video. Trust the pipeline. Revise the ones that matter. Most perform best on first edit.
  • Posting all 30 in one week. Spread them across the month. The algorithm rewards consistency.
  • Ignoring the bottom 3. The duds teach you what your audience doesn't want. Read the report.
