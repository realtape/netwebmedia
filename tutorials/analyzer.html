1. What it checks

  • Search engine basics — page titles, descriptions, sitemaps, and the small tags search engines need.
  • AI readiness — whether AI tools like ChatGPT can pull clean answers from your pages.
  • Speed — how fast your pages load. How your images are sized. How smooth the page feels.
  • Content gaps — pages your competitors rank for that you don't.
  • Accessibility — image descriptions, heading order, color contrast, keyboard use.
  • Conversion signals — where your buttons sit, how heavy your forms are, whether you look trustworthy.

3. What your score means

  • 90 to 100 — excellent. Only small tune-ups needed.
  • 70 to 89 — solid. A few high-impact fixes put you in excellent.
  • 50 to 69 — real gaps. You are losing traffic. Fixable in 4 to 8 weeks.
  • Below 50 — broken basics. Consider a rebuild. We can help.

4. Fixing what it finds

Every flagged issue comes with:

  • What it is, in plain English.
  • Why it matters (traffic? rankings? sales?).
  • How to fix it, step by step or with a code snippet.
  • How long the fix should take.
  • Priority: do this first, this second, this later.

5. Limits of the free tool

Great as a starting point. Here is what it does not do:

  • It scans up to 50 pages. Our paid audit scans everything.
  • It does not look at which sites link to you. Paid audit does.
  • It does not talk to your customers. Paid audit includes 3 customer calls.
  • It does not fix the issues for you. That is us, or your team.

What success looks like

  • Within 90 seconds: a 20- to 40-page PDF with your score and a ranked list of fixes.
  • Within 30 days of fixing the top 5 red items: a measurable bump in speed and rankings.
  • Within 90 days: higher traffic, fewer visitors bouncing, more leads captured.

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Reading the PDF once and forgetting it. Print the top-priority fixes. Tape them to your wall. Work through them.
  • Fixing the yellow stuff before the red stuff. Red first. Always.
  • Checking only your site. Run it on two or three competitors. You'll see what you're up against and what they missed.
