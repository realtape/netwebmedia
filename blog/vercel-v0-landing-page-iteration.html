Vercel v0 Is Making Landing Page Iteration Cycles Absurd

By Marcus Chen · April 15, 2026 · 6 min read

Vercel's v0 turns a text description into a production-quality React landing page in 30 seconds. For marketing teams who used to wait two weeks for dev to build a new variant, it's a step-change in iteration speed.

The new test cadence

Teams that adopted v0 seriously are running landing page tests at 10x their old cadence. Where a quarter used to yield 4 landing page experiments, it now yields 40. The winners compound.

The code v0 generates is real React using Tailwind and shadcn/ui — not a toy sandbox. You can check it into your repo, review it, and ship it to production without a rewrite.

Where marketers still need help

v0 still needs a good prompt. 'Landing page for a SaaS' produces something generic. 'Landing page for a security SaaS targeting CISOs with a hero that leads with a specific customer quote and a below-the-fold trust bar' produces something usable.

The valuable skill isn't code. It's writing tight, opinionated briefs that give v0 something specific to render.

Our internal workflow

  • Strategist writes a 200-word brief per variant.
  • v0 generates 5 options.
  • Marketer picks the winner and tweaks in v0's live editor.
  • Dev reviews and merges.
  • Ships same day, every day.
v0 doesn't replace designers or devs. It replaces the week-long back-and-forth where marketing and dev try to align on what 'good' looks like.
