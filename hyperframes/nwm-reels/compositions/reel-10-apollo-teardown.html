4 layers. What they nail and what leaks pipeline.
LAYER 01 / ABOVE THE FOLD
HERO: WHAT WORKS.
THE HOOK
"Find your perfect leads" — outcome-first headline
Apollo leads with the customer outcome, not the product feature. Reinforced by social proof (10,000+ companies) above the CTA. This is correct. The CTA is "Get started free" — low friction, high intent.
// Assessment: Strong. Outcome headline + social proof + free tier = best practice.
LAYER 02 / SOCIAL PROOF
10K logos — too much, too fast
Apollo shows a logo wall of 10,000 companies. The sheer volume dilutes trust. Specificity beats scale: "HubSpot grew outbound 40% with Apollo" beats 9,999 anonymous logos.
// Fix: swap 3 logos for outcome-specific case study blurbs.
LAYER 03 / FEATURE SECTION
Feature-led, not problem-led
The mid-page section lists features (email sequencing, dialer, data enrichment) instead of mapping each feature to a pain. A B2B buyer thinks in pain, not capability.
// Fix: reframe each feature as: "Problem → Apollo solves it by..."
LAYER 04 / BOTTOM CTA
Three CTAs at the bottom. One too many.
"Get started free" + "Book a demo" + "See pricing" at the fold break. Decision fatigue. The funnel leaks here. One CTA wins — the highest-intent one (demo).
// Fix: kill "see pricing," push "book a demo" as the primary.
THE VERDICT
WHAT WORKS
VS. WHAT LEAKS.
✓
Outcome-first hero headline — correct, on-brand, high clarity
✗
Logo wall dilutes trust — specificity beats volume
✗
Feature section = wrong frame — map to pain, not capability
✗
Three bottom CTAs = leak — one CTA converts, three confuse
B+
Strong foundation.
3 fixable leaks costing ~20% CVR.
