1. What this actually means

A normal part-time CMO gives you a senior marketer for 5 to 10 hours a week. They hand you a strategy document. Then you try to execute it with your small team — usually badly.

NWM CMO is different. You get a strategic marketer PLUS our execution team — designers, writers, ad buyers, video editors, workflow engineers. One package. You are not translating strategy into execution. We own the whole chain.

Is this a fit?

Best for companies doing $500,000 to $20 million a year who don't have a full-time VP of Marketing, or who have a junior marketer who needs a senior leader on top.

3. Month 1: the strategy sprint

1

Week 1: listen.

Kickoff call (2 hours). We interview 5 to 8 of your stakeholders. We get read access to your data. We tear down your top 3 competitors.

2

Week 2: diagnose.

Full audit of your brand, channels, customer cost, customer value, holes in your buyer journey, team strengths, and current tools.

3

Week 3: plan.

Who is your perfect customer. How do we talk to them. What channels we'll bet on. The 90-day plan. How we'll spend the budget.

4

Week 4: kick off execution.

One-hour readout to your leadership. You approve. Month 2 execution begins.

4. Months 2 to 12: doing the work

Your CMO leads a pod built for your specific needs. A typical Scale pod:

  • 1 CMO — strategy, executive reviews, internal alignment
  • 1 content strategist — search, blog, email
  • 1 designer — ads, one-page sites, brand assets
  • 1 paid ads specialist — Meta and Google
  • 1 analytics person — dashboards, tracking, CRM cleanup

We work in weekly sprints. Kickoff Monday. Review Friday. You get a shared Slack or Teams channel with the whole pod.

5. Monthly scorecards and quarterly reviews

Monthly scorecard — first business day of each month. A one-page PDF with sales pipeline impact, customer cost, return on ad spend, brand awareness signals, traffic, leads, qualified leads, big wins, big losses, and next month's focus.

Quarterly review — 90-minute executive call with you and your leadership. We present results against the 90-day plan. Reset goals. Adjust budget.

7. What results to expect

Honest expectations based on 40+ engagements:

  • Months 1 to 3 — strategy done, execution begins. Early wins from new ad creative and email.
  • Months 4 to 6 — lead volume grows. 1.5 to 2 times more qualified leads is typical.
  • Months 7 to 12 — compounding kicks in. Search traffic up. Ads more efficient. Brand signals rising. 2 to 4 times the sales pipeline. Customer cost down 15 to 30%.
This is a 12-month commitment

Marketing compounds. If you are not thinking 12 months, do not hire any CMO. Minimum: 6 months.

8. How we hand off when you hire in-house

When you are ready to bring marketing in-house, we help you do it. We help recruit your VP of Marketing. Write the job description. Interview candidates. Overlap for 60 days and hand off everything. No drama. No lock-in.

9. Is this right for you?

Yes if you do $500,000 to $20 million a year, are growing 20% or more, don't have a VP of Marketing, and have a team of 5 to 50.

No if you do under $500,000 (too early — focus on the product), have a strong VP already (we will step on their toes), or move slowly (we move faster than you can approve things).

What success looks like

  • Within 30 days: a clear strategy, a 90-day plan, and visible execution beginning.
  • Within 6 months: 1.5 to 2 times more qualified leads, measurable lift in at least two channels.
  • Within 12 months: 2 to 4 times the sales pipeline, compounding organic traffic, and a cleaner reporting rhythm your executives actually use.

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Hiring a CMO before you have product-market fit. If customers aren't buying from word-of-mouth, marketing won't fix it. Fix the product first.
  • Expecting a miracle in 90 days. Month 3 is when the compounding starts. Month 12 is when it shows up loud.
  • Splitting channels across three agencies. Nobody owns the outcome. One pod, one CMO, one invoice wins.
