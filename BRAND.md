## 2. Brand Personality

### We Are

| Adjective | Rationale |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Precise** | We use specific numbers, named tools, and measurable outcomes. Vagueness is a trust signal for generalists. We are not generalists. |
| **Direct** | We lead with the point. No warming-up, no hedging. The audience is a time-starved founder — respect that. |
| **Confident** | We do not qualify claims with "might" or "could." If we don't believe it, we don't say it. |
| **Technical** | We name the stack (ChatGPT, Perplexity, GHL, GA4). This separates us from agencies that say "AI" as a buzzword. |
| **Relentless** | We work on the reader's behalf. The copy, the design, the follow-up — all of it has a job to do. Nothing is decorative. |
| **Premium-practical** | The design is polished and dark, like a Bloomberg terminal or Linear.app. But the pricing is built for SMBs, not Fortune 500. The tension between "looks expensive" and "accessible cost" is intentional. |

### We Are Not

| Anti-adjective | Why we actively avoid it |
|----------------|--------------------------|
| **Hype-y** | "Revolutionary," "disruptive," "game-changing" — these are red flags for sophisticated buyers. We let outcomes do the work. |
| **Cute** | Emojis in headlines, playful mascots, casual jokes in error messages. We are not a consumer app. |
| **Vague** | "We help businesses grow" tells the prospect nothing. Every claim is specific, every CTA is concrete. |
| **Apologetic** | Softening language ("we try to," "hopefully," "if you're interested") signals low confidence. Cut it. |
| **Corporate** | Passive voice, jargon-laden mission statements, committee-approved language. We write like a sharp human, not a procurement doc. |
| **Derivative** | We do not copy HubSpot's friendly-orange aesthetic or GoHighLevel's bro-funnel energy. We are neither. |

## 3. Voice & Tone

### 5 Voice Principles

---

**Principle 1: Lead with the outcome, not the feature.**

| | Example |
|--|---------|
| DO | "Get cited by ChatGPT and Perplexity — we build the content infrastructure that earns those citations." |
| DON'T | "Our AI content engine uses advanced NLP to optimize for answer-engine visibility." |

Rationale: SMB founders don't buy features. They buy revenue, time, and peace of mind.

---

**Principle 2: Name things exactly.**

| | Example |
|--|---------|
| DO | "We integrate your CRM with WhatsApp, GA4, and your ad accounts in 72 hours." |
| DON'T | "We connect your marketing tools for a seamless workflow." |

Rationale: Specificity is credibility. Every vague phrase can be sharpened with a named tool, a number, or a timeframe.

---

**Principle 3: Use second person, active voice, present tense.**

| | Example |
|--|---------|
| DO | "You get a strategy call, a 90-day roadmap, and your first campaign live — in the first two weeks." |
| DON'T | "Clients are provided with a comprehensive onboarding experience that includes strategic planning." |

Rationale: "You" puts the reader in the frame. Active verbs compress the sentence and add energy.

---

**Principle 4: Earn the exclamation point.**

| | Example |
|--|---------|
| DO | "That's $3,400/month in agency fees you're no longer paying." |
| DON'T | "Amazing results await you! Get started today!" |

Rationale: Excitement should come from the claim, not the punctuation. If the sentence needs an exclamation point to seem exciting, the sentence is not exciting enough.

---

**Principle 5: End with motion.**

| | Example |
|--|---------|
| DO | "Book your free audit. We'll show you exactly where you're leaving revenue on the table." |
| DON'T | "Feel free to reach out if you have any questions." |

Rationale: Every piece of copy is part of a funnel. It should make something happen next.

---

### Tone Shifts by Context

| Context | Tone | Key adjustments |
|---------|------|-----------------|
| **Sales page / landing page** | Confident, urgent, direct | Short paragraphs. Benefit-led headers. Social proof near every CTA. Price visible. |
| **Blog article** | Expert, educational, slightly conversational | Longer sentences permitted. First person ("we found," "in our experience"). Cite sources. No hard sell until the end. |
| **Customer email (onboarding / update)** | Warm, clear, operational | Start with what happened or what's next. No fluff opener. Use bullet lists for action items. |
| **Error message / system notice** | Calm, honest, actionable | State what went wrong in plain language. Tell them exactly what to do. Never blame the user. |
| **Social post (X / Instagram)** | Sharp, punchy, slightly provocative | 1-2 sentences max for hook. No throat-clearing. A stat or a contrarian take beats a feel-good affirmation every time. |

### Pairing Rules
- **Orange on Navy Dark**: passes WCAG AA and AAA — primary combination for all CTAs.
- **White on Navy Dark**: passes WCAG AAA — primary combination for all body text.
- **Blue Accent on Navy Dark**: passes WCAG AA — acceptable for secondary actions and links.

- **Never pair Orange on Orange-Light** or Navy on Navy Dark without a border — insufficient contrast.

## 6. Typography System

### Fonts
- **Display / UI headings:** Poppins (Google Fonts) — weights 600, 700, 800
- **Body / Interface:** Inter (Google Fonts) — weights 300, 400, 500, 600, 700
- **Fallback stack:** `-apple-system, BlinkMacSystemFont, "Segoe UI", sans-serif`
- **Monospace (code, price badges):** `ui-monospace, "Cascadia Code", monospace`

Both Poppins and Inter are already loaded in `styles.css`. They are the permanent brand fonts. Do not introduce new typefaces without a full brand review.

Rationale for this stack: Poppins has geometric personality and strong weight contrast for display use — it carries authority without feeling stiff. Inter is the gold standard for screen readability at small sizes. Together they produce the "premium-practical" tension we want.

### Type Scale

```

│  H4            Poppins   24px               600   1.30  0            │

```

### Scale Usage Rules

| Role | Use |
|------|-----|
| **Display** | Hero section title only — used once per page maximum |
| **H1** | Page title or primary section hero. One per page. |
| **H2** | Major section headers (Services, Pricing, About) |
| **H3** | Card titles, feature headers, blog section breaks |
| **H4** | Sub-feature titles, sidebar headers, step labels |
| **Body Large** | Hero description paragraph, intro paragraphs, testimonial quotes |
| **Body** | All standard paragraph copy |
| **Body Small** | Feature list items, card descriptions, form helper text |
| **Caption** | Image captions, timestamps, attribution lines |
| **Label / Tag** | Section eyebrows (uppercase, tracked), badge text, stat labels |
| **Code** | Price codes (`Carlos26`), inline code references, promo badges |

### Pairing Rule
Poppins headlines + Inter body is the only approved pairing. Do not set paragraphs in Poppins — it is too wide at body sizes. Do not set headlines in Inter — it lacks the display gravity Poppins provides.

### Web Font Loading Strategy

Place these in the `<head>` of every page, before the stylesheet link:

```html
<link rel="preconnect" href="https://fonts.googleapis.com">
<link rel="preconnect" href="https://fonts.gstatic.com" crossorigin>
<link rel="preload" as="style"
  href="https://fonts.googleapis.com/css2?family=Inter:wght@400;500;600;700&family=Poppins:wght@600;700;800&display=swap">
<link rel="stylesheet"
  href="https://fonts.googleapis.com/css2?family=Inter:wght@400;500;600;700&family=Poppins:wght@600;700;800&display=swap">
```

Load only the weights you use. The current `styles.css` loads Inter 300–900 and Poppins 400–900 — that is six extra weights per font causing unnecessary render-blocking payload. Trim to the weights listed above.

## 7. Logo Usage

### Files

| File | Format | Use |
|------|--------|-----|
| `assets/nwm-logo.svg` | SVG vector mark | Favicon, app icon, social profile image, standalone mark |
| `assets/nwm-logo-horizontal.svg` | SVG horizontal lockup | Nav bar, email header, co-branding lockups, proposals |
| `assets/images/netwebmedia-logo-lockup.png` | PNG raster | Legacy fallback only — prefer SVG wherever possible |

### Clear Space
The minimum clear space around any logo version is equal to the cap-height of the "N" in the wordmark. Call this unit X.

```
        X
    ┌───────────────────────────────┐
  X │                               │ X
    │   [NWM MARK]  NetWebMedia    │
    │                               │
    └───────────────────────────────┘
        X
```

Nothing — text, image, edge, decorative element — enters this zone.

### Minimum Sizes

| Context | Minimum width |
|---------|---------------|
| Digital — horizontal lockup | 120px |
| Digital — mark only | 24px |
| Print — horizontal lockup | 35mm |
| Print — mark only | 8mm |
| Favicon / app icon | 16px (mark only, no wordmark) |

### Approved Color Versions

| Version | When to use |
|---------|-------------|
| Full color on dark (orange mark + white wordmark on navy/dark bg) | Default — all digital surfaces with dark backgrounds |
| Full color on light (orange mark + navy wordmark on white/light bg) | Print, light-mode partner documents, proposals on white |
| White monochrome (all white) | Dark photography backgrounds, sponsorship materials where color is unavailable |
| Navy monochrome (all `#012169`) | Light backgrounds when color is not available |
| Reversed (white on orange) | Emergency use only — orange CTA banner backgrounds |

### Six Logo Don'ts

**1. Do not stretch or distort the proportions.**
The mark and wordmark have a fixed aspect ratio. If your layout requires it to stretch, the layout is wrong.

**2. Do not recolor with non-brand colors.**
Purple, teal, green, gradient overlays, photo-composite colorizations — none of these are approved. Monochrome white or navy are the only alternates to the full-color version.

**3. Do not place the logo on busy photography or complex backgrounds.**
If the background has medium-to-high visual noise (textures, patterns, multiple colors), use a solid dark overlay or the white version on a contrasting surface.

**4. Do not add effects: shadows, glows, bevels, or emboss.**
The logo SVG has no drop-shadow. Do not add one. The glow effects on the site are UI elements — they do not apply to the lockup itself.

**5. Do not reduce below minimum size.**
At sizes below 24px (digital) or 8mm (print), the mark loses legibility. Use the standalone mark at small sizes and never attempt to display the full lockup below minimums.

**6. Do not use the mark in running copy or inline with text.**
The mark is not an icon in a sentence. It is a signature. It lives in headers, footers, and standalone placements — not inline with body text.

### Co-branding Rules
When appearing alongside a partner logo, the NetWebMedia lockup occupies at minimum 80% of the partner logo's width. Use a vertical rule or a space of 2X as separator. Both logos sit on a neutral background. Do not let partner brand colors contaminate the clear space around our mark.

## 8. Iconography

### Icon Library: Lucide

**Chosen library: Lucide** (https://lucide.dev)

Rationale: Lucide is a maintained fork of Feather Icons with a significantly larger icon set (1,400+). Stroke-only. Clean, geometric, minimal. It matches the linear precision of the Poppins/Inter typographic system exactly. Phosphor has more character variants (thin/bold/duotone) which would create inconsistency risk when used by multiple contributors. Heroicons has fewer icons and is more opinionated about Tailwind usage. Lucide wins on consistency + coverage + maintenance activity.

### Usage Rules

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| Default stroke width | 1.5px |
| Icon size in UI (nav, buttons, list items) | 16–20px |
| Icon size in feature cards / section headers | 24px |
| Icon size in hero / large feature callouts | 32–40px |
| Color in buttons | Inherits button text color |
| Color in nav | `var(--text-secondary)` default, `var(--nwm-orange)` on active/hover |
| Color in cards | `var(--nwm-orange)` for primary, `var(--nwm-blue)` for informational |

### Import Pattern (CDN, no build tool required)

```html
<!-- Per page, in <head> -->
<script src="https://unpkg.com/lucide@latest/dist/umd/lucide.js"></script>
<!-- In HTML -->
<i data-lucide="arrow-right" width="16" height="16"></i>
<!-- After DOM ready -->
<script>lucide.createIcons();</script>
```

For pages where performance matters (landing pages), download the specific icons you need and inline the SVG. Do not load the full Lucide bundle on every page.

### Banned Patterns
- Do not mix Lucide with Font Awesome, Bootstrap Icons, or Material Icons on the same page.
- Do not use filled (solid) variants from other libraries — Lucide is stroke-only and the visual language must stay consistent.
- Do not animate icons in ways that distract from copy — subtle rotation on a spinner is fine; jiggling a feature icon is not.

## 9. Imagery & Photography

### What Looks Right

**Subject matter:** Working professionals — founders, marketers, sales leads — in modern office/remote environments. Screens showing real interfaces (dashboards, analytics, chat interfaces). Close-ups of hands on keyboards. Urban environments with depth (blurred city backgrounds). Data visualization aesthetics — charts, graphs, glowing nodes.

**Lighting:** High contrast. Hard shadows or dramatic edge lighting on subjects. Dark backgrounds preferred. Rim lighting (subjects lit from behind/side against dark backgrounds) photographs well against the brand's navy environment.

**Composition:** Rule of thirds. Leave the right third clear for text overlay. Avoid centered subjects on photos that will be used as backgrounds. Depth of field — blurred backgrounds, sharp subject.

**Color grade:** Slightly cool-to-neutral with warm highlights. Desaturate mid-tones. If in doubt: pull blue shadows, add slight orange warmth to highlights. This maps to the brand's navy/orange palette naturally.

### Stock Photo Traps — Avoid These Exactly

- Smiling people shaking hands in generic conference rooms (looks like a 2009 insurance website)
- Overhead flat-lay shots of notebooks, coffee cups, and phones (lifestyle blog aesthetic, not premium agency)
- Multicultural stock groups pointing at whiteboards with forced enthusiasm
- Any photo where the only AI reference is a glowing brain or robotic hand (overused to the point of self-parody)
- Blue-tinted "technology" photos with circuit board textures or binary code
- People looking at tablets at an angle that clearly shows nothing on screen

### AI-Generated Imagery Guardrails

We use AI-generated images for blog illustrations, social posts, and section backgrounds — not for people photography (too many artifact risks and authenticity concerns with clients).

**Midjourney / fal.ai prompt template:**
```
[subject description], cinematic dark background, deep navy and orange color grading,
rim lighting, professional photography aesthetic, high contrast, volumetric lighting,
subtle lens flare, 8k, --ar 16:9 --style raw --v 7
```

**Approved subjects for AI generation:** Abstract data visualization, glowing UI mockups, futuristic workspace environments (no people), geometric brand pattern backgrounds, hero section visual elements.

**Do NOT generate:** Faces of people (use real photography for people), competitor logos or interfaces, anything that could be mistaken for a real client result.

### Illustration Style

When using illustrations (blog, tutorials, product explainers): flat vector style, dark background, orange and blue accent colors only, Poppins-like geometric shapes. Reference: Linear.app's illustration style, Vercel's blog imagery.

Do not use: hand-drawn/sketchy illustration, pastel/light-background illustration, 3D cartoon characters, stock illustration libraries that look like they came from Freepik.

## 12. Motion & Animation

### Philosophy
Animation communicates state changes and guides attention. It is never decoration. Every animation on the site should answer the question: "Does this help the user understand what just happened or what to do next?"

### Duration Tokens

```css
/* Add to :root in styles.css */
--duration-instant:  100ms;   /* Micro: focus rings, opacity flickers */
--duration-fast:     200ms;   /* UI feedback: button hover, nav link */
--duration-default:  300ms;   /* Standard: card hover, dropdown open (already --transition) */
--duration-medium:   500ms;   /* Page elements entering viewport */
--duration-slow:     700ms;   /* Hero text sequences */
--duration-ambient: 2000ms;   /* Loops: pulse dots, floating squares */
```

### Easing Curves

| Name | Value | Use |
|------|-------|-----|
| Standard | `cubic-bezier(0.4, 0, 0.2, 1)` | All UI interactions (already `--transition`) |
| Enter (decelerate) | `cubic-bezier(0.0, 0.0, 0.2, 1)` | Elements entering the screen |
| Exit (accelerate) | `cubic-bezier(0.4, 0.0, 1, 1)` | Elements leaving the screen |
| Bounce | `cubic-bezier(0.34, 1.56, 0.64, 1)` | CTAs on hero, floating stat cards — use sparingly |

### What Animates vs. What Does Not

| Animates | Does NOT animate |
|----------|-----------------|
| Card hover states (translateY, shadow) | Brand logo |
| CTA button hover (translateY, glow) | Body paragraph text |
| Navigation link underlines | Section headers on scroll (no parallax) |
| Hero text fade-in on load | Table rows |
| Typewriter on hero gradient text | Form labels |
| Particle canvas / floating squares (ambient) | Error messages (static, urgent) |
| Orbital rings on hero robot (ambient) | Pricing numbers |
| Dropdown open/close | Loading states in buttons (exception: spinner icon only) |
| Modal enter/exit | |
| Scroll-triggered section reveals (opacity + translateY) | |

### Typewriter Effect — When It Applies

The current hero typewriter cycles through positioning variations of the headline ("for Growing US Brands." / "for Bilingual SMBs." / etc.). This pattern is approved for exactly one use: the hero H1's gradient text suffix that cycles through audience/outcome variants.

**Rules for the typewriter pattern:**
1. Only on hero sections — one per page.
2. Cycle interval: minimum 2.5 seconds per phrase. Do not rush it.
3. Only on gradient text (orange-to-orange-light). Never on white text.
4. The static part of the headline is more important than the animated part. The animation supports — it does not lead.
5. Do not use a typewriter effect on any copy that contains a CTA or a price.

### Scroll-Triggered Reveals
Use `fadeInUp` (already defined as a keyframe in styles.css) for all elements that should enter the viewport with animation. Target: 60px of vertical travel, 0 to 1 opacity, staggered by 100–150ms between sibling elements.

### Transactional Email (receipts, confirmations, password resets)

- Subject line: `[NetWebMedia] [Action] — [specific detail]`. Example: `[NetWebMedia] Payment confirmed — Launch plan, April 2026`
- Lead with the action result, not a greeting.
- Single CTA maximum.
- No promotional copy in the body. The footer may include one link to the dashboard.
- No imagery beyond the logo. Text + CTA + footer only.
- Tone: calm, clear, operational. See Voice Principle 3.

### Marketing Email (campaigns, newsletters, retainer updates)

- Subject line: benefit or provocation, max 50 characters. No exclamation points.
- Preview text: 85–100 characters, extends the subject rather than repeating it.
- Structure: hook paragraph (2–3 sentences) → value content → single primary CTA → secondary text link
- One primary CTA per email. A second CTA is permitted only in the P.S. or footer.
- Images: one hero image maximum above the fold. Hosted on netwebmedia.com CDN.
- Personalization: use `{first_name}` in the opener. Never in the subject line — it reads as spam.
- Unsubscribe: one-click, prominent in footer. Required by CAN-SPAM and GDPR.

### Bilingual Emails
Do not send bilingual emails with both languages in the same message. Segment by language preference. If language is unknown, send in English with a link: "Prefiere leer en español → [link]"

## 16. Accessibility Commitments

### Baseline
WCAG 2.2 Level AA is the minimum for every page, component, and email. We target AAA on text contrast wherever it does not require a design compromise.

### Contrast Table — All Brand Color Pairs

| Foreground | Background | Hex pair | Ratio | AA pass | AAA pass | Notes |
|-----------|-----------|---------|-------|---------|---------|-------|
| White `#FFFFFF` | Navy Dark `#010F3B` | — | **18.1:1** | PASS | PASS | Primary text combination |
| White `#FFFFFF` | Navy Primary `#012169` | — | **14.5:1** | PASS | PASS | Card headers |
| White `#FFFFFF` | Navy Mid `#01306E` | — | **11.8:1** | PASS | PASS | Secondary sections |
| Orange `#FF671F` | Navy Dark `#010F3B` | — | **6.3:1** | PASS | PASS | CTAs, section labels |
| Orange `#FF671F` | White `#FFFFFF` | — | **3.0:1** | FAIL small | PASS large | Only for text 18px+ bold |
| Orange Light `#FF8C42` | Navy Dark `#010F3B` | — | **5.2:1** | PASS | FAIL | Acceptable for large text, not small |
| Blue `#4A90D9` | Navy Dark `#010F3B` | — | **4.8:1** | PASS | FAIL | Secondary actions, links in body |
| Text Secondary `#C8D4E6` | Navy Dark `#010F3B` | — | **11.1:1** | PASS | PASS | Body paragraphs |
| Text Muted `#7B8FAD` | Navy Dark `#010F3B` | — | **4.6:1** | PASS (large) | FAIL | Use only for captions/labels at 13px+ |
| Success `#00B894` | Navy Dark `#010F3B` | — | **7.2:1** | PASS | PASS | Success messages |
| Warning `#FDCB6E` | Navy Dark `#010F3B` | — | **9.8:1** | PASS | PASS | Warning banners |
| Error `#E17055` | Navy Dark `#010F3B` | — | **5.1:1** | PASS | FAIL | Error messages at 16px+ |

**Hard rule:** Never use Text Muted (`#7B8FAD`) for body copy or any text conveying meaning in a CTA context. It barely passes AA for large/label use only.

### Keyboard & Screen Reader Principles

1. **Skip link:** Every page has `<a class="skip-link" href="#main">Skip to main content</a>` as the first element in `<body>`. Already implemented — do not remove.
2. **Focus indicators:** Every interactive element has a visible focus ring. Default: `outline: 3px solid var(--nwm-orange); outline-offset: 2px;`. Never `outline: none` without a custom replacement.
3. **Semantic HTML:** Use `<nav>`, `<main>`, `<section>`, `<article>`, `<aside>`, `<footer>` correctly. Every section that appears in navigation should have a corresponding `id` attribute.
4. **ARIA where needed:** Hamburger button: `aria-label="Menu"` and `aria-expanded`. Cart button: `aria-label="View cart"`. Icon-only buttons always need `aria-label`.
5. **Images:** All meaningful images have descriptive `alt` text. Decorative images (glow orbs, background particles) have `alt=""` and `aria-hidden="true"`.
6. **Form inputs:** Every `<input>` and `<textarea>` has an associated `<label>` or `aria-label`. Never use `placeholder` as the only label.
7. **Typewriter animation:** The `.typewriter` element must have `aria-live="polite"` so screen readers announce text changes without interrupting ongoing reading.
8. **Color alone:** Never use color as the only way to convey state. Error fields need a text error message, not just a red border.
9. **Motion:** Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`. Wrap all non-essential animations:
   ```css
   @media (prefers-reduced-motion: reduce) {
     *, *::before, *::after {
       animation-duration: 0.01ms !important;
       transition-duration: 0.01ms !important;
     }
   }
   ```
